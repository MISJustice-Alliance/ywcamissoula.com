My name is Tyrone Nuno. I am the father of Elvis Ryland Nuno, and I submit this letter as a supplemental witness grievance in support of ODC File No. 25-147. I am aware that my son Elvis filed his initial grievance on or about July 2025, that he submitted multiple supplemental submissions (Supplemental #1, August 2025; Supplemental #3, September 2025), that Bryan Tipp responded through Tipp, Colburn, Lockwood, P.C. on September 23, 2025, and that Elvis subsequently filed a Request for Review on or about November 18, 2025. I have reviewed those materials. My purpose in writing is to provide firsthand corroborating testimony that directly rebuts Mr. Tipp's characterization of my son and adds evidence that, to my knowledge, the ODC has not yet considered.

---

## I. Qualifications as a Witness

I am uniquely positioned to provide evidence relevant to this disciplinary matter. My son Elvis has lived with me and has served as my caregiver throughout the entire period of the Missoula prosecution, approximately 2018 through January 2021. I was personally present at in-office meetings with Mr. Tipp and attended pretrial hearings with my son. Because of our living arrangement, I was able to observe the day-to-day impact of the case on Elvis, to witness firsthand how Mr. Tipp communicated — or failed to communicate — with our family, and to participate directly in actions related to the case, including my own formal complaint against Officer Ethan Smith in March 2018.

---

## II. Mr. Tipp's Conduct Toward the Client and Family

Over the course of my son's representation, I observed a pattern of conduct by Mr. Tipp that I regarded as deeply troubling. In my view, Mr. Tipp was rarely a forceful advocate for my son's position. When Elvis raised concerns that appeared legitimate and material — concerns I shared — Mr. Tipp would dismiss or minimize them rather than engage with them seriously. This was consistent enough that it was noticeable to me as a lay observer with no legal training.

What I found particularly unprofessional was a specific pattern I personally witnessed: when my son would push back on positions Mr. Tipp was taking, Mr. Tipp would at times bring me privately into his office and apply pressure on me to, in turn, pressure Elvis to submit to Mr. Tipp's demands. This placed me in an untenable position. Rather than advocating for his client, Mr. Tipp was enlisting the client's elderly father as a pressure mechanism against that client. This is not how I understand an attorney's duty of loyalty to function, and I found it deeply inappropriate.

This observation is consistent with Supplemental Submission #3 filed by Elvis, which documents that Mr. Tipp additionally pressured Elvis to surrender passwords for electronic devices seized under what the complaint describes as an unlawful warrant, and required Elvis to surrender First Amendment rights as a condition of the non-prosecution agreement negotiated with the prosecutor — matters my son describes as never having been adequately explained or fairly counseled to him.

---

## III. The Ethan Smith Complaint and Its Complete Disregard by Mr. Tipp

I want to bring to this office's direct attention a significant episode that I am personally responsible for and that Mr. Tipp, by every indication, completely ignored.

In March 2018, I filed a formal written complaint with Sergeant Dustin DelRidge of the Missoula Police Department against Officer Ethan Smith (Badge #345). In that complaint — which I am attaching hereto — I stated that beginning in June 2017, Officer Smith appeared without invitation at my ex-wife Ellie Nuno's residence, and thereafter made repeated attempts to contact both of us by phone, letters, and messages regarding Elvis. I described this as incessant harassment of our family members over a matter we had nothing to do with. My complaint characterized Officer Smith's behavior as "greatly exaggerating whatever this situation is," "simply fishing for information," and an attempt to "embarrass, humiliate, and intimidate" my son. I requested all contact with our family cease immediately.

The result of that complaint was significant: following my direct conversations with Sergeant DelRidge, Officer Ethan Smith was removed from any matter involving Elvis Nuno and E'Lise Chard. According to the case records I am aware of, this removal was due to the appearance of impropriety and unbecoming conduct.

This was material information directly relevant to Elvis's defense, and to the broader narrative of investigative misconduct surrounding the prosecution. Yet at no point did Mr. Tipp:

- Ask me about my complaint against Officer Smith;
- Interview me regarding the substance of my interactions with Officer Smith or Sergeant DelRidge;
- Investigate why the case was reassigned after Smith's removal;
- Use my complaint or its outcome to challenge the investigative integrity of the prosecution; or
- Take any action reflecting any awareness of this information as useful to Elvis's defense.

Supplemental Submission #1, filed by Elvis in August 2025, specifically documents this failure at length. That submission identifies the transfer from Smith to Detective Brueckner as a critical missed opportunity for the defense. It notes that Smith was removed for "obvious appearance of impropriety" due to his close personal relationship with E'Lise Chard, and that this should have prompted Mr. Tipp to immediately investigate the background and potential conflicts of the replacement detective — Detective Brueckner — who, it is alleged, served on the Board of Directors of the YWCA of Missoula, the very organization at the center of this prosecution.

I can confirm from personal experience that the red flags surrounding Officer Smith were known, documented, and communicated — including to persons connected to the case. Mr. Tipp had access to this information. He was defending Elvis at the time my complaint was filed and Officer Smith was removed. That he never once asked me about any of it, despite my attendance at meetings and my direct role in forcing a change of investigator, is, in my view, indefensible.

---

## IV. Response to Mr. Tipp's Characterization of My Son

In his September 23, 2025 response to this office, Mr. Tipp characterized my son's grievance as "founded upon lies, misrepresentations and falsifications" and described it as "the product of Mr. Nuno's attempted extortion, harassment and intimidation of Mr. Tipp." I find these statements deeply unfair and, to the extent they purport to describe what happened during the representation, inconsistent with my own observations.

I was present. I attended meetings. I watched how Mr. Tipp interacted with Elvis, and I saw the effects of the representation on my son and our family. Nothing in my experience suggests that Elvis fabricated his concerns. To the contrary, my observations corroborate the core factual assertions he has made: that Mr. Tipp did not adequately listen to us, that he failed to investigate important evidence, that he appeared more concerned with institutional accommodation than with zealous defense, and that the 18-to-20-month duration of the case was caused at least in part by an unwillingness to press harder for resolution.

It is my understanding that Elvis's November 2025 Request for Review identifies three distinct ethical violations: active suppression of critical information, an unjustified delay that benefitted institutions over the client, and refusal to provide basic professional advocacy. These align with what I personally observed. The characterization of a grievance-filing — an explicitly protected process — as "extortion" strikes me as a serious mischaracterization. No honest person who attended those meetings and watched what my family went through would describe my son's attempt to seek professional accountability as dishonest or extortionate.

---

## V. The Human Cost That "Dismissal" Does Not Capture

Mr. Tipp's response to ODC argues that dismissal of all charges with prejudice is proof of competent and vigorous defense. I respectfully ask this office to consider what the 18-to-20-month proceeding actually cost our family before that dismissal was reached.

Elvis lost his career. He has been subjected to ongoing police harassment and what I believe was intentional infliction of emotional distress. He has been financially bankrupted. His First Amendment rights were effectively silenced during the period of the case. He has been left traumatized, fearful of law enforcement, and unable to trust institutions that failed him at every level.

Our entire family suffered with him. We were brought to our financial and emotional breaking point. My ex-wife and I, in our older years, were harassed by a police officer who had no business contacting us, and that harassment was documented and raised with supervisors — yet the defense counsel who was being paid to protect our son never even acknowledged it as evidence.

In my observation, the case came to an end only after my ex-wife and I directly demanded that it be resolved. That is not a description of vigorous advocacy producing an optimal result. That is a description of a family reaching its limits and forcing a conclusion that should have come much sooner.

A dismissal does not undo what was destroyed during the pendency of the prosecution. It does not recover the civil rights claims that were not preserved. It does not address the failure to use known witness evidence. And it does not justify characterizing a son's attempt to hold counsel accountable as dishonest.

---

## VI. Summary of Issues for ODC Consideration

Based on my personal observations and in support of Elvis's existing filings (ODC File No. 25-147 and all supplemental submissions), I respectfully ask this office to evaluate:

1. **Whether Mr. Tipp improperly used a client's family member as a pressure mechanism** to compel the client's submission to counsel's preferred strategy, contrary to the duty of loyalty owed to the client directly.

2. **Whether Mr. Tipp failed to investigate and use material witness evidence** — specifically my March 2018 complaint against Officer Ethan Smith and the resulting removal of that officer — despite its obvious relevance to the defense narrative and to the Brady/Giglio analysis of the subsequent investigator's conflicts.

3. **Whether Mr. Tipp failed to interview available witnesses with direct and material knowledge**, including me, despite my repeated presence at meetings and my direct involvement in actions affecting the investigative history of the case.

4. **Whether the Supplemental #1 finding — that Smith's removal should have prompted investigation into why the case was reassigned to Detective Brueckner (alleged YWCA board member) — is borne out by the documented record**, including my complaint and the removal outcome.

5. **Whether Mr. Tipp's characterization of Elvis's grievance as lies and attempted extortion is consistent with the facts**, as observed by a family member present throughout the representation.

6. **Whether the eventual dismissal of criminal charges is an adequate answer** to the question of whether the representation met professional standards during the 18-to-20-month period that preceded it, in light of evidence of ignored witnesses, uninvestigated conflicts, and documented family pressure tactics.
