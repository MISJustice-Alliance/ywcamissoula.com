## BACKGROUND

The matters in this grievance are cases captioned State of Montana v. Elvis Ryland Nuno, Cause No. CR-2018-981, Missoula County Justice Court, Judge Marie Andersen presiding, and Cause No. DC-18-587, in Missoula County Fourth Judicial District Court, Judge Leslie Halligan presiding. The charges filed were Stalking, a misdemeanor; Stalking, 2nd or Subsequent Offense, a felony; and Stalking - Victim Under Protection of Restraining Order, a felony. Mr. Tipp noticed his appearance in Justice Court on September 12, 2018. Bond Conditions were set, and the case was transferred to the District Court on September 28, 2018. The State's Petition to Revoke Defendant's Release was filed also on September 28, 2018, due to a violation of the conditions of release. The Motion to Temporarily Modify Conditions of Release and was filed on December 5, 2018, and the Unopposed Motion on December 20. The State filed the Amended Information on July 30, 2019, updating the alleged offenses. Defendant's Pre-trial Motion Related to Amended Information, Motion to Suppress and Brief in Support and Motion to Exclude Evidence Under ER 406 & Brief in Support were filed November 6, 2019. The Motion to Dismiss was filed on December 12, 2019, and the Order of Dismissal was entered by the Court on December 13. A Motion to Dismiss with Prejudice was filed January 5, 2021, and the Order was entered the same day.

## DISCUSSION

### Your Grievance

ODC summarizes your grievance as follows.

You hired Mr. Tipp to represent you in the above criminal defense matter in June 2018. Your grievance provides lengthy allegations of various Montana Rules of Professional Conduct ("MRPC") violations by Mr. Tipp. For example, Mr. Tipp failed to pursue your timely civil claims when he advised you against filing civil claims, while aware of ongoing harassment by a police officer and YwA affiliates, directly causing the expiration of statutes of limitations for those claims. Mr. Tipp knew of the ongoing harassment and economic damages incurred as a result. Mr. Tipp put the interests of the prosecution before his client's interests, and his approach contributed to a lack of scrutiny and action by the YWCA, the Missoula Police Department and the Missoula City Prosecutor's Office. You attach Facebook messages, correspondence, and AI legal analysis documents in support of your allegations.

You provide two supplemental submissions with additional allegations of violations and lis. For example, you submit that Mr. Tipp was negligent in his representation of you by failure to identify conflicts of interest or file any substantive motions, in addition to your previous allegations. You question his competence, diligence and communication with you and allege misconduct on his part.

Your final supplemental submission focuses on Mr. Tipp's pressure on you and your family to provide your passwords for electronic devices seized under an unlawful warrant, despite professional and legal risks to you and your 1st Amendment rights. Further, Mr. Tipp was aware of the violations of your rights and failed to render substantive assistance or acknowledge those violations. You provide a legal analysis, the fee agreement and billing statements.

### The Attorney's Response

ODC summarizes Mr. Tipp's response as follows.

Mr. Torrance L. Coburn provides the response on behalf of Mr. Tipp. The crux of your grievance is that Mr. Tipp failed to pursue civil claims on your behalf for alleged civil rights violations. Mr. Tipp represented you in relation to criminal charges brought against you and not for alleged civil rights violations, per the fee agreement. Mr. Tipp provides the agreement and filings that set forth the charges.

Mr. Tipp reiterated this fact to you on several occasions, and you understood that Mr. Tipp was not retained to pursue civil claims on your behalf and that he and his firm would not do so. You reference both seeking outside counsel and speaking to outside counsel regarding your claims. Mr. Tipp's June 30, 2025, email was sent in response to your threatening email and harassing texts, and he sets out that he advised you that they would not prosecute your claims, that they did not perceive your claims were well supported by the law and the existing facts, and that your claims were impractical from a potential recovery standpoint. His letter further points out that you were advised as to the existence of potential statutes of limitations and that you were encouraged to find counsel to assist you, which you reported you had done in July of 2020.

You allege a violation of Rule 1.1, questioning Mr. Tipp's competence. You were initially charged with two counts of Felony Stalking, one count of Misdemeanor Stalking and one count of Violation of a Protection Order. Through Mr. Tipp's representation, all the criminal charges against you were dismissed, with prejudice. This dismissal was a result of Mr. Tipp's competent defense, which included the filing of several substantive motions on your behalf, attached.

You argue that Mr. Tipp was not competent because he failed to timely pursue civil claims on your behalf; however, he was not retained by you to pursue civil claims, and Mr. Tipp repeatedly told you that you would have to obtain other counsel to do so, which was acknowledged by you and supported by the attached email exchanges.

Further, you question Mr. Tipp's competence in relation to Detective Brueckner's involvement in the matter, noting the detective's ties to the YWCA. Mr. Tipp's competent representation of you meant that a trial was unnecessary, and it was at trial where those ties would have been material and potentially cast doubt on the investigation.

You question Mr. Tipp's diligence, alleging that he failed to file motions, including motions to dismiss and to suppress evidence, which is demonstrably false. The motions he filed led to the best possible resolution, dismissal of the case. You take issue with the fact that you were required to post bail and were subject to GPS monitoring; however, given that you violated conditions of release the same day that you were placed on GPS supervision, such bail and conditions were not patently unreasonable, especially given the nature of the charges, and a challenge would have proven unsuccessful. You may disagree with some of the strategy, but the strategy led to the dismissal, with prejudice.

In regard to Rule 1.4 allegations, this is also demonstrably false as Mr. Tipp and you met on many occasions and discussed your defense at length. Mr. Tipp communicated with you regularly by email on your case, and such communications dispute your allegations that Mr. Tipp failed to discuss potential civil claims. Some of these emails are attached. Additionally, you were specifically advised during in-person meetings that time limitations existed for potential civil actions, and if you were not aware of those associated statutes of limitations, he wonders why you were seeking to retain outside counsel for such matters during the time Mr. Tipp represented you on your criminal charges.

You allege Rule 1.7, Conflict of Interest, violations, whereby you appear to allege that Mr. Tipp acted in the interest of the prosecution, rather than you. Again, he achieved a dismissal of all charges, and it is not a conflict of interest for him to focus on defending against the criminal charges for which he was retained rather than the civil claims for which he was not retained. No factual or legal basis exists for your grievance.

### Your Reply

ODC summarizes your reply as follows.

You disagree with Mr. Tipp's characterization of your grievance, which is not about whether he was retained for civil representation but that Mr. Tipp actively advised against civil action and suppressed your understanding of urgent civil deadlines, resulting in the expiration of all viable civil rights claims worth millions in potential recovery. You provide detailed additional analysis of Mr. Tipp's failures on your behalf.

### Analysis

The Montana Rules of Professional Conduct ("MRPC") govern lawyer conduct in Montana. ODC's burden of proof in attorney disciplinary proceedings requires clear and convincing evidence establishing a violation of the MRPC. In other words, there has to be compelling evidence that an attorney's conduct violated a Rule. Your grievance implicates Rule 1.1, Competence; Rule 1.3, Diligence; Rule 1.2, Scope of Representation and Allocation of Authority Between Client and Lawyer; and Rule 1.4, Communication, MRPC.

Rule 1.1, Competence, says that a lawyer shall provide competent representation to a client which requires the legal knowledge, skill, thoroughness and preparation reasonably necessary for the representation. Rule 1.3, Diligence, says a lawyer shall act with reasonable diligence and promptness in representing a client. You allege that Mr. Tipp showed lack of competence and diligence by failing to understand and address the civil implications of the violations of your constitutional rights. Mr. Tipp asserts that his job was to address your criminal charges, which he did successfully. ODC sees no evidence that Mr. Tipp violated these Rules in the representation that he was hired to provide, as indicated by the fee agreement.

Although not addressed by you, also implicated is Rule 1.2, Scope of Representation and Allocation of Authority Between Client and Lawyer, which says a lawyer shall abide by a client's decisions concerning the objectives of representation (e.g., to go to trial, take a plea or testify) and shall consult with the client as to the strategy for accomplishing those objectives. The lawyer then may take such action on behalf of the client as necessary. In your case, for example, you wanted or allege that Mr. Tipp should have filed motions or taken certain actions, but the Rules give your attorney authority to choose the strategy even if you disagree and even if it is unsuccessful in the end, which was not the case here. Mr. Tipp's strategy to focus on the criminal matter, his retained objective, and discourage your confrontation with other parties did not preclude you from finding another attorney to take on your civil claims. Again, you may disagree with his strategy, but that does not mean he acted unethically, and it does not violate Rule 1.2.

Rule 1.4, Communication, says a lawyer shall keep the client reasonably informed about the status of the matter and promptly comply with reasonable requests for information. You allege Mr. Tipp failed to communicate with you meaningfully regarding civil rights deadlines. Mr. Tipp describes his communications with you about your criminal matter and asserts that he did communicate with you about your civil claims, declined to participate in them and encouraged you to seek other counsel for those claims, if you so chose. ODC finds there is ample evidence of Mr. Tipp's compliance with Rule 1.4.

Regarding your other allegations not discussed herein, ODC determines there is insufficient evidence of an ethical violation and refrains from restating the allegations.

## DETERMINATION

In view of the foregoing, ODC concludes this matter does not warrant disciplinary action and therefore dismisses your grievance subject to your right to request review by the Commission on Practice.
