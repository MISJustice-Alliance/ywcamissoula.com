# MT Bar Complaint ODC No. 25-147 - Bryan Tipp of Tipp, Colburn, Lockwood, P.C. (July 2025)

{% include "../../.gitbook/includes/odc-25-147-related-misjustice-alliance-case-file.md" %}

## I. Grounds for Complaint

I allege that Bryan Tipp, while representing me in criminal proceedings (State v. Nuno, Missoula County), committed legal malpractice and violated the Montana Rules of Professional Conduct by:

1. **Failure to Pursue Timely Civil Claims**: Despite clear evidence of civil rights violations, Mr. Tipp advised against filing civil claims to "give the matter time," directly causing statute of limitations expirations for:
   * 42 U.S.C. Section 1983 claims (3-year statute)
   * Defamation claims (3-year statute)
   * IIED claims (3-year statute)
2. **Conflict of Interest**: Prioritizing criminal defense expediency over civil remedies, violating Rule 1.7(a)(2) MRPC
3. **Lack of Diligence**: Failing to investigate civil options despite documented evidence, breaching Rule 1.3 MRPC

## II. Factual Background

* **2018-2020 Representation**: Mr. Tipp defended against stalking charges (dismissed with prejudice)
* **Explicit Advice**: He discouraged civil action, stating "give the matter time"
* **Statute Expiration**: Key deadlines lapsed by 2020 without civil filings

## III. Legal and Ethical Violations

| Violation                      | MRPC Rule             | Evidence                                             |
| ------------------------------ | --------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------- |
| Failure to pursue civil claims | Rule 1.1 (Competence) | Caused irreversible loss of claims                   |
| Conflict of interest           | Rule 1.7(a)(2)        | Advised passivity benefiting prosecutorial interests |
| Lack of diligence              | Rule 1.3              | Ignored documented harassment and damages            |

## IV. Requested Action

1. **Investigate** Mr. Tipp's malpractice and ethical breaches
2. **Sanction** him for violations, including potential restitution
3. **Refer** to Commission on Practice for formal proceedings
