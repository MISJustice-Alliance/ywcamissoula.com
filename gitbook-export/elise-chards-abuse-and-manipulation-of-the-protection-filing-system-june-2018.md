# E'Lise Chard's Abuse and Manipulation of the Protection Filing System (June 2018)

## Overview

Analysis of E'Lise Chard's alleged manipulation of the protection order filing system in June 2018.

A core issue is whether the June 12, 2018 petition was filed in retaliation for a June 3, 2018 complaint to YWCA leadership. That complaint is framed as protected petitioning activity under the First Amendment.

## Documented Issues

### June 2018 YWCA complaint (protected petitioning activity) and alleged retaliation

Allegation: On or about **June 3, 2018**, Elvis Ryland Nuno submitted a written complaint to YWCA Missoula leadership (President Deirdre Flaherty and Executive Director Cindy Weese). The complaint alleged employee misconduct by **E’Lise Chard** and **Rebecca Pettit**.

Key allegations in the complaint:

* Pattern of false police reporting beginning in Washington (2016) and continuing in Montana (2017–2018).
* Vindictive intent tied to family/personal history involving Danielle Chard.
* Contact with MPD Officer **Ethan Smith** in June 2017 under the guise of YWCA business.
* Knowingly false statements and fabricated evidence presented to Officer Smith.
* Attempts to portray Nuno’s IT background as a “cyber crime spree.”
* Use of YWCA position/resources to give personal harassment institutional legitimacy.

Alleged downstream impacts described:

* A near year-long pattern of aggressive, off-the-record harassment of Nuno and his parents.
* Escalation contributing to severe mental health deterioration in Nuno’s mother.

Requested action (as alleged):

* Investigation by YWCA leadership.
* Immediate cessation protocol.
* Accountability for involved staff.

Additional fact asserted:

* Complaint included a **$400 donation** and supportive language about YWCA’s mission.

Retaliation theory asserted:

* YWCA did not respond or investigate.
* YWCA allegedly provided the complaint letter and donation to E’Lise Chard.
* E’Lise allegedly used those materials when filing a protection order petition **nine days later** (June 12, 2018).
* The protection order court later found the YWCA complaint was protected speech.
* The record attributes that finding to Judge Sam Warren at the protection order hearing.

Allegation: A third-party character reference was sent to YWCA describing nonviolent, nonthreatening conduct and disputing the narrative used to justify escalation. The statement is framed as **exculpatory** because it directly contradicts key assertions attributed to E’Lise Chard.
