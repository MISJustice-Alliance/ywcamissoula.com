# MPD Officer Misconduct Series: 2018 Complaint by Tyrone Nuno Regarding Officer Ethan Smith

This entry documents a March 2018 complaint filed by Tyrone Nuno, father of Elvis Ryland Nuno, concerning repeated, unwanted contact and alleged harassment by Missoula Police Department Officer Ethan Smith (Badge #345) directed at Mr. Nuno's family. The complaint is presented here as a primary-source record within the MPD Officer Misconduct series, capturing in Tyrone's own words how a family was drawn into an investigation they had "nothing to do with."

## Background and Timeline

According to the complaint, Officer Ethan Smith began contacting the Nuno family in June 2017 by appearing at the residence of Tyrone's ex-wife, Ellie Nuno, to ask questions about their son, Elvis. Over the months that followed, Smith allegedly made "at least a half dozen" further attempts to contact both Tyrone and Ellie through visits, phone calls, messages, and letters.

Tyrone states that he initially cooperated and spoke with Smith once, but emphasizes that the officer's contact continued despite his having no information to offer. The described pattern aligns with broader archive materials documenting how Missoula PD and associated actors repeatedly contacted the Nuno parents during the early Montana phase of the case, often outside formal investigative channels.

## Full Text of the Complaint (March 2018)

> Sergeant Dustin Delridge  
> Missoula Police Department  
> 435 Ryman St.  
> Missoula, MT 59802  
>   
> To whom it may concern,  
> This complaint is regarding officer Ethan Smith (Badge #345). Beginning in June of 2017 officer Smith showed up at my ex wife's (Ellie Nuno) residence at [REDACTED] asking questions about my son Elvis Nuno. Since that time he has made at least a half dozen other attempts to contact me and my ex wife. He has incessantly harassed myself, Tyrone Nuno, and ex wife, Ellie Nuno, with phone calls, messages, and letters. At first I was cooperative with him and I spoke with him briefly once which was fine. But since then he has continued calling me and leaving messages for me and my ex wife. This is highly unprofessional and unethical conduct for a police officer to confront someone's family members over a matter they have nothing to do with. I would like to be left alone seeing as how I don't know anything, and it causes a great deal of tension in my family. It seems to me like he is greatly exaggerating whatever this situation is and has no reason to believe my son is actually doing anything wrong, but is simply fishing for information and is trying to embarrass, humiliate, and intimidate my son. I do not want to be part of this, and am incredibly angered by this officer's unbecoming behavior and incessant harassment. Please, I am asking that I be left alone, and that all attempts to contact myself and my family members cease and desist immediately.  
>   
> Thank you very much for your time and consideration.  
> Sincerely,  
> Tyrone Nuno

## Key Allegations and Characterization of Misconduct

Tyrone characterizes Officer Smith's conduct as "highly unprofessional and unethical," emphasizing that the officer was "confront[ing] someone's family members over a matter they have nothing to do with." He notes that the repeated contacts caused "a great deal of tension" in his family and describes Smith's behavior as "incessant harassment" toward both himself and his ex-wife.

Crucially, Tyrone asserts that Smith "has no reason to believe" Elvis was "actually doing anything wrong," and instead is "simply fishing for information and is trying to embarrass, humiliate, and intimidate" his son. This framing is consistent with other documents in the archive that describe Missoula officers relying on informal surveillance and family pressure rather than grounded, evidence-based investigation.

## Cease-and-Desist Demand and Institutional Response

The complaint culminates in an explicit cease-and-desist request: Tyrone asks that he "be left alone" and that "all attempts to contact" himself and his family "cease and desist immediately." This language clearly communicates non-consent to further contact and places the department on notice that the family experiences the officer's outreach as harassment rather than assistance.

Archive summaries indicate that, after this complaint and subsequent conversations between Tyrone and Sergeant Delridge, Officer Smith was removed from any case involving Elvis Nuno and E'Lise Chard due to the "appearance of impropriety and unbecoming conduct" toward the Nuno parents. Despite that reassignment, later charging documents reportedly continued to rely on Smith's investigative work, underscoring the importance of preserving this complaint as a contemporaneous record of objection to his methods.
