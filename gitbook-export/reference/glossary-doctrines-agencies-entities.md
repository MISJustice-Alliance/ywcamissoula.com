# Glossary (doctrines, agencies, entities)

### Executive snapshot

This glossary standardizes the vocabulary used across the site: key **legal doctrines**, key **agencies**, and recurring **entities**. It is written for fast skimming and quoteable definitions.

{% hint style="info" %}
This site publishes **allegations** and links to **primary records**.

This glossary is a terminology aid, not legal advice and not a court finding.
{% endhint %}

### Doctrines and legal concepts

#### 42 U.S.C. § 1983 (Section 1983)

A federal civil-rights statute that allows suits against government actors for deprivation of federal rights **under color of state law**.

See also:

* [Missoula §1983 misconduct: civil rights violations and related claims (2015-2025)](../civil-rights-violations-and-related-claims-2015-2025.md)

#### Monell municipal liability

A §1983 doctrine for municipal liability when a constitutional violation is caused by a municipal policy, custom, or failure-to-train amounting to deliberate indifference.

See also:

* [Missoula §1983 misconduct: civil rights violations and related claims (2015-2025)](../civil-rights-violations-and-related-claims-2015-2025.md)

#### Qualified immunity

A defense that can shield individual officials from §1983 damages unless the violated right was "clearly established" at the time.

#### Absolute prosecutorial immunity

A doctrine that immunizes prosecutors from damages for activities intimately associated with the judicial phase of prosecution (e.g., initiating and pursuing charges).

#### Brady violation

Suppression of **material exculpatory** evidence by prosecutors.

See also:

* [Prosecutorial misconduct and Brady issues (evidence index)](../additional-evidence-and-documentation/missoula-1983-misconduct-mpd-prosecutors-ywca-of-missoula-expanded-history/evidence-related-to-civil-rights-violations-of-mr-nuno-2015-2025/prosecutorial-misconduct-and-brady-issues-evidence-index.md)

#### Giglio issue

Suppression of **material impeachment** evidence (e.g., credibility problems, inducements, or benefits).

See also:

* [Prosecutorial misconduct and Brady issues (evidence index)](../additional-evidence-and-documentation/missoula-1983-misconduct-mpd-prosecutors-ywca-of-missoula-expanded-history/evidence-related-to-civil-rights-violations-of-mr-nuno-2015-2025/prosecutorial-misconduct-and-brady-issues-evidence-index.md)

#### Malicious prosecution (civil-rights framing)

A claim theory used in some circuits (often via the Fourth Amendment) when legal process is allegedly misused to prosecute without probable cause and with resulting harm.

#### Abuse of process

A tort concept where legal process is used for an ulterior purpose not intended by the process.

#### First Amendment retaliation

Government retaliation for protected speech or petitioning activity, where the retaliation would chill a person of ordinary firmness.

See also:

* [YWCA of Missoula's Role in First Amendment Violations Against Mr Nuno (2018-2025)](../ywca-of-missoulas-role-in-first-amendment-violations-against-mr-nuno-2018-2025.md)

#### Fourth Amendment (search and seizure)

Constitutional protection against unreasonable searches and seizures. In practice, disputes often focus on probable cause, warrant scope/particularity, and reasonableness.

See also:

* [Fourth Amendment evidence (entry, seizure, and digital search)](../additional-evidence-and-documentation/missoula-1983-misconduct-mpd-prosecutors-ywca-of-missoula-expanded-history/evidence-related-to-civil-rights-violations-of-mr-nuno-2015-2025/fourth-amendment-evidence-entry-seizure-and-digital-search.md)

#### Fourteenth Amendment (due process and equal protection)

Due process protects against deprivation of life, liberty, or property without adequate process. Equal protection prohibits certain forms of discriminatory or arbitrary enforcement.

See also:

* [Full Analysis of Fourteenth Amendment Equal Protection and Due Process Violations (2015-2025)](../full-analysis-of-fourteenth-amendment-equal-protection-and-due-process-violation.md)

#### Heck / favorable termination

A rule that can bar certain §1983 claims if success would imply the invalidity of a still-valid conviction or sentence.

#### Unconstitutional conditions

A doctrine that limits government from conditioning benefits or favorable treatment on surrendering constitutional rights.

#### RICO (Racketeer Influenced and Corrupt Organizations Act)

A federal statute allowing criminal and civil liability for participating in an enterprise through a pattern of racketeering activity.

{% hint style="warning" %}
RICO has specific statutory elements and pleading requirements.

This site treats RICO framing as an **allegation and analytical hypothesis**, not an adjudicated fact.
{% endhint %}
