# Fishing Expedition via Facebook Account Data Dump Search Warrant (2018)

## Introduction

The prosecution's Facebook search warrant requesting "_all_ data contained in \[Mr. Nuno's] account" represents a paradigmatic **Fourth Amendment violation** through an overbroad "fishing expedition" that lacked the constitutional particularity requirement.

## I. Fourth Amendment Violations in the Facebook Search Warrant

### A. Constitutional Requirements for Digital Search Warrants

The Fourth Amendment requires search warrants to "particularly describe... the persons or things to be seized". For digital evidence, courts have recognized that the particularity requirement "assumes even greater importance."

### B. The Facebook Warrant as an Unconstitutional Fishing Expedition

1. **Lack of Particularity** - Failed to specify what evidence was sought
2. **General Warrant Characteristics** - Constituted illegal "general warrants"
3. **Chilling Effect on Protected Speech** - Impermissible First Amendment impact

### C. Complete Absence of Evidence

The invasive Facebook search found **no evidence of even mentioning E'Lise Chard**, confirming both the absence of probable cause and the improper fishing expedition nature of the warrant.

## II. Bryan Tipp's Recognition of the Constitutional Violation

Bryan Tipp "made reference to this in the motion to dismiss he ultimately filed," indicating awareness of the constitutional problems.

## III. Tipp's Contradictory Advice Against Civil Action

Despite recognizing and arguing the constitutional violations, Tipp simultaneously advised Mr. Nuno against pursuing civil rights claims.

### Professional Negligence

* Montana Rule 1.1 (Competence) - Failed to provide necessary legal knowledge
* Montana Rule 1.3 (Diligence) - Allowed statute of limitations to expire
* Montana Rule 1.4 (Communication) - Failed to inform about civil rights claims

## Expired Claims Due to Tipp's Negligence

Total loss from Tipp's negligence regarding the Facebook warrant alone exceeds **$1.4 million**.
