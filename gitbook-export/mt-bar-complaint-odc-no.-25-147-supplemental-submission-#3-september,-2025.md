# MT Bar Complaint ODC No. 25-147: Supplemental Submission #3 (September, 2025)

## Summary

This supplemental submission provides comprehensive analysis of Attorney Bryan Tipp's failures in representation, expanding on the original complaint with additional evidence of professional conduct violations.

## Key Violations Documented

### Montana Rules of Professional Conduct Violations

1. **Rule 1.1 - Competence**: Failed to recognize and challenge constitutional violations
2. **Rule 1.3 - Diligence**: Failed to pursue available legal remedies
3. **Rule 1.4 - Communication**: Failed to inform client of critical legal options
4. **Rule 3.1 - Meritorious Claims**: Failed to file motions with strong legal basis

## Timeline of Representation Failures

- Mid 2018: Charges filed, Tipp retained
- 18 months: Prosecution deferred without constitutional challenges
- 12 months additional: Case dismissed with prejudice
- Total: 30 months of proceedings that could have been avoided

## Damages Summary

Total documented losses exceeding $3.4 million including:
- Career destruction and lost income
- Legal fees for inadequate representation
- Ongoing professional reputation damage
