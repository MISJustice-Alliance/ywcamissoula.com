### Meadowlark shelter evictions (Oct 2021, third-party reporting)

External source (media):

* [Four families being evicted from the YWCA Meadowlark due to a growing waitlist (MontanaRightNow, Oct 2021)](https://www.montanarightnow.com/missoula/four-families-being-evicted-from-the-ywca-meadowlark-due-to-a-growing-waitlist/article_02d85c24-2884-11ec-863a-43bfdd6b42c2.html)

Key reported facts (as stated in the article):

* **Four families** were being evicted from YWCA Meadowlark due to **capacity** and a **growing waitlist**.
* A single mother reported she had until **Oct. 29** to fund a hotel stay or find housing.
* The family included three children, ages **10, 9, and 7**.
* The mother reported she was **not given a move-out date** at intake.
* The mother reported staff said families were “safe” if they were **working toward housing**.
* The YWCA later told families who had been there **more than 90 days** they had **two weeks to a month** before eviction.
* YWCA Executive Director **Cindy Weese** attributed the decision to the waitlist and the inability to offer long-term shelter while giving more families a chance to stabilize.
* The article states the Rapid Rehousing program’s **payment cap** did not match Missoula rental prices, leaving families at risk of homelessness.

Why this matters to this evidence map:

* **Child endangerment risk**: eviction into cold-season conditions can foreseeably increase exposure risks for minors.
* **Notice / reliance issues**: the reported “no move-out date” intake practice and later deadline change is a repeatable fact pattern to verify via written policies, logs, and notices.
* **Funding and compliance questions** (needs records): when publicly funded shelter/rehousing programs exit families due to capacity, the critical facts become the written termination standards, grievance rights, and outcome reporting.
