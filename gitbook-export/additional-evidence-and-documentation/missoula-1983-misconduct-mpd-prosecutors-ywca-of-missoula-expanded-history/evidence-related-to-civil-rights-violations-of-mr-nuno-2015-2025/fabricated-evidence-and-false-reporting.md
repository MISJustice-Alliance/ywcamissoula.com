### E’Lise Chard reports and narrative inflation

Common patterns visible in the record:

* Claims of criminal/drug history that do not match the underlying history.
* “Threat” framing that does not match the actual communication content.
* Alleged ongoing proximity contradicted by location/monitoring data.

### Danielle Chard narrative construction

Key contradictions repeatedly show up as:

* “One date” framing versus a long documented relationship history.
* Cross-country stalking framing versus periods of direct communication.
* Allegations about social posts that do not match archived content.

### Mental health and reliability issues (handled as an evidentiary question)

If you use psychological dynamics, keep it tight.

* Focus on **observable behaviors** and objective contradictions.
* Use experts for any diagnostic claims.
* Avoid overstating beyond what the record supports.
