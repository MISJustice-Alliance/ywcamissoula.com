# P.S. Email Addendum (August 14, 2018)

## Overview

This section documents a critical discovery exhibit: an August 14, 2018 email sent by a third party (anonymized as **P.S.**) to the YWCA of Missoula general inbox, criticizing employee E'Lise Chard's conduct and supporting Elvis Nuno. Rather than being handled as an internal personnel complaint, the email was forwarded by E'Lise Chard herself into police and victim-services channels within nineteen minutes of receipt.

The exhibit and its accompanying addenda raise public-accountability questions about:

- YWCA complaint handling and institutional independence
- The treatment of favorable and contradictory information in prosecution-adjacent channels
- Potential witness framing and credibility handling
- The relationship between YWCA staff and law enforcement actors

## Context

This exhibit sits within a broader documented pattern in which complaints to YWCA about staff conduct were followed by no visible internal remedy, continued employment of accused staff, and escalating police and prosecutorial activity involving YWCA-linked actors. The P.S. email is significant because it provides a concrete, documented mechanism showing how a third-party complaint was redirected from an organization's general inbox into the criminal-case ecosystem by the employee who was the subject of the complaint.
