# Public Addendum: P.S. Email, YWCA Complaint Handling, and Retaliation Concerns

## Overview

This public addendum explains why the August 14, 2018 P.S. email and its forwarding chain matter to the public record concerning YWCA complaint handling and the escalation that followed Elvis Nuno's complaints about YWCA-linked conduct. The core public-interest point is narrow: a third-party complaint sent to YWCA's general inbox about employee conduct appears in discovery as having been forwarded by the accused employee into police and victim-services channels rather than appearing, on the available record, to remain in an internal grievance process.

This version is revised for publication on a public evidentiary site and keeps the sender anonymized as **P.S.**. It is intended to document an accountability concern and preserve a clear factual record, not to make a final legal finding on retaliation or malicious prosecution.

## What the Exhibit Shows

The discovery record shows that P.S. emailed `ywca@ywcaofmissoula.org` on August 14, 2018 using the subject line "Attn: Regarding conduct of employee E'Lise Chard." In that message, P.S. described Elvis Nuno as kind, respectful, generous, and non-abusive, stated that E'Lise Chard had been causing harassment and trouble for Elvis and his family, and urged YWCA to address the issue and "see what is really going on."

The same record shows that nineteen minutes later E'Lise Chard forwarded the message to Detective Arianna Adams and Kim Harvey, stating: "Here is an email to the YWCA, about me, on Elvis' behalf. Not sure if you want this, since it's purportedly not from Elvis, but I figured I'd send it anyway." Kim Harvey later forwarded the same chain to Arianna Adams on August 23, 2018, preserving the email in a law-enforcement or victim-services record stream.

## Why This Raises Public Concerns

For public readers, the main significance is not that the exhibit alone resolves the entire dispute, but that it shows how a complaint to YWCA about one of its employees may have been rerouted. Instead of clearly appearing as an internal personnel or grievance matter, the message appears in the available record as prosecution-adjacent material circulated by the employee who was the subject of the complaint.

That creates a legitimate public question about whether YWCA's intake and complaint systems were operating independently or whether criticism of staff could be redirected into outside enforcement channels. In public-accountability terms, that is a governance and process issue even before any court reaches a conclusion on retaliation or malicious prosecution.

## Relationship to Earlier Complaints

The P.S. email did not arise in isolation. Earlier materials show that Elvis Nuno had already sent a formal grievance to YWCA leadership on June 3, 2018, alleging misconduct by E'Lise Chard and Rebecca Pettit, describing prior police-related escalation, and asking YWCA to investigate and stop the conduct.

Existing chronology materials further describe a broader pattern in which complaints to YWCA were followed by no documented internal remedy, continued involvement of YWCA-linked actors, police escalation, and eventual criminal proceedings that later ended in dismissal with prejudice. Read in that context, the P.S. email matters because it appears to provide an additional example of criticism reaching YWCA and then moving into the criminal-case ecosystem instead of being visibly handled as an internal complaint.

## Public Questions Raised

This exhibit supports a number of narrow, document-based questions suitable for a public evidentiary page.

- Did YWCA leadership or administrative staff receive and review the original email before it was forwarded externally?
- Was the message logged as a complaint about employee conduct or handled outside ordinary grievance procedures?
- Were any internal investigative or corrective steps taken after receipt of either the June 2018 grievance or the August 2018 P.S. email?
- Why does the available record show the complaint being forwarded by the employee named in the complaint to police and victim-services contacts?

These questions do not require the site to prove a legal claim. They are public-interest questions about process integrity, institutional neutrality, and whether a nonprofit advocacy organization maintained clear boundaries between staff-accountability functions and law-enforcement channels.

## Public Framing of Retaliation Concerns

For publication purposes, the strongest framing is restrained: the exhibit appears consistent with a retaliation concern because criticism of staff was not visibly handled as a neutral grievance and instead appears to have been redirected into an external investigative stream. That is a fair public description of what the document suggests without overstating what it conclusively proves.

The forwarding language is part of why the exhibit is notable. E'Lise characterized the email as being "on Elvis' behalf" while simultaneously acknowledging it was only "purportedly" so, which can reasonably be read as recasting independent third-party speech into something potentially attributable to Elvis himself. For public readers, that makes the record relevant not only to complaint handling, but also to how supportive communications may have been framed once they entered outside channels.

## What This Addendum Does Not Claim

This public addendum does not claim that the exhibit alone proves a complete conspiracy, proves malicious prosecution, or establishes liability against every institution or person mentioned in the broader file set. It also does not attempt to answer legal questions about elements, immunity, or statutes of limitation, which depend on fuller official records and attorney analysis.

The narrower and more supportable conclusion is that the P.S. email is a documented example of a complaint to YWCA about employee conduct being forwarded into police/victim-services channels by the employee named in the complaint, and that this is relevant to public evaluation of YWCA's independence, grievance handling, and relationship to the larger escalation described elsewhere in the record.
