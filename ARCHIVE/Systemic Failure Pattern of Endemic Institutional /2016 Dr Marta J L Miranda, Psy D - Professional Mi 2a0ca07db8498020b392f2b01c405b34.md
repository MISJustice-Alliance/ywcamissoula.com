# Detailed Incident Analysis

## Nature and Context of Court-Ordered Evaluation

The September 19, 2016 evaluation occurred within a coercive legal framework where Mr. Nuno, at age 31, faced his **first serious criminal charge** - a 3rd-degree domestic violence allegation. Dr. Miranda held a **monopolistic position**, described as "the only doctor who had the required credentials" for this court-ordered assessment. This exclusive access created significant leverage over a vulnerable defendant with limited alternatives.

The evaluation was required as part of a **plea agreement**, making Dr. Miranda's conclusions determinative for Mr. Nuno's criminal case resolution. This high-stakes context amplifies the significance of any professional misconduct, as fabricated psychological evidence directly impacts constitutional rights to due process and equal protection.

## Specific Professional Misconduct Allegations

### 1. **Fabrication of Patient Statements**

The most egregious allegation involves Dr. Miranda including **"a shocking number of statements that were never said"**. Critically, Mr. Nuno had **audio recordings of all sessions**, providing verifiable evidence to contradict the psychological report. This fabrication was not mere interpretation or clinical judgment but demonstrable falsehood - creating **forensic evidence of professional fraud**.

The fabricated statements were strategically selected to support an **unsupported diagnosis**, suggesting deliberate manipulation rather than incompetence. Under Washington's Rules of Professional Conduct and APA Ethics Code Standard 2.04, psychologists must base professional opinions on **"established scientific and professional knowledge"** - a standard violated by the inclusion of fabricated patient statements.

### 2. **Misrepresentation of Family Interviews**

Dr. Miranda conducted interviews with **Tyrone Nuno** (father) and **David Nelson** (family friend/father figure) without proper disclosure of her identity, credentials, or the purpose of the interviews. Both family members subsequently reported that their words were **"grossly misrepresented"** and taken "literally the opposite" of their intended communication.

This conduct violates **APA Ethics Code Standard 3.10** regarding informed consent and Standard 4 concerning confidentiality limits. The failure to disclose the forensic nature of the interviews and subsequent misrepresentation constitutes **fraudulent inducement** - making any resulting testimony inadmissible and professionally unethical.

The family members' **"remorse"** and feeling **"quite livid"** about their participation demonstrates the manipulative nature of Dr. Miranda's approach, suggesting intentional deception to obtain statements that could be distorted to support predetermined conclusions.

### 3. **Hostile Relationship with Treating Therapist**

**Torr Lindberg**, Mr. Nuno's treating therapist of **four years**, was **"dumbfounded by the evaluation"** and challenged its conclusions. This professional disagreement is particularly significant given the longitudinal therapeutic relationship versus Dr. Miranda's single-session assessment.

Even more suspect was that her diagnosis of “narcissistic personality disorder” is defined by a subject meeting at least five of nine specified criteria, of which her report only outlined three criteria, and of those three Mr Lindberg had not observed during the course of treating Mr Nuno. However when Mr Lindberg pointed out the inconsistencies in her evaluation Dr Miranda was dismissive, stating that “everything is explained in the evaluation” and insinuating that he simply lacked the qualifications to understand or make a valid assessment.

Dr. Miranda's **"hostile responses"** to the treating therapist's inquiries about the evaluation demonstrate unprofessional conduct and unwillingness to engage in peer consultation - a violation of APA Ethics Code Standard 2.04 regarding bases for scientific and professional judgments. The refusal to explain or defend clinical conclusions to a professional colleague suggests awareness of the evaluation's deficiencies.

### 4. **Unsupported Diagnostic Conclusions**

Dr. Miranda diagnosed **Narcissistic Personality Disorder** with **"no discernible rational"** and **"no substantive evidence of requisite symptoms"** per DSM criteria. This violates fundamental principles of psychological assessment under **APA Ethics Code Standard 9.01**, which requires that psychologists base opinions on **"information and techniques sufficient to substantiate their findings" and that the subject meets at least five of nine specified criteria (of which the evaluation only provided three).**

The arbitrary diagnostic conclusion appears designed to support legal proceedings rather than reflect clinical reality - transforming forensic psychology into **advocacy rather than objective assessment**. This role confusion violates the **Specialty Guidelines for Forensic Psychology**, which emphasize **"integrity, impartiality and fairness"**.

### 5. **Financial Exploitation and Coercion**

Dr. Miranda **nearly doubled** her original quote of $2,000-$2,400, charging **"over $4,000"** and forcing payment **"or face jail time"**. This exploitative pricing structure takes advantage of the defendant's vulnerable position and constitutes potential **extortion under color of legal authority**.

Additionally, she charged **additional fees** for correcting her own errors while **"still mostly failing to correct"** the documented problems. This financial manipulation violates basic principles of professional integrity and fair dealing.

### 6. **Obstruction and Retaliation**

Following detailed documentation of errors, Dr. Miranda **refused to correct demonstrable falsehoods**, only addressing **"typos and grammatical errors"**. She **threatened legal action** against further complaints and established a **final deadline** (November 10, 2016) with threats to close the case.

This obstructive conduct violates **APA Ethics Code Standard 1.04** regarding ethical violations and Standard 1.05 concerning reporting violations. Rather than addressing legitimate professional concerns, Dr. Miranda engaged in intimidation tactics designed to suppress accountability.

### **1. Audio Evidence Ignored**

No indication exists that DOH investigators **reviewed the audio recordings** - the most compelling evidence of fabricated statements. This represents a basic failure of investigative thoroughness and suggests predetermined conclusions.

The investigation failed to assess Dr. Miranda's conduct against established **APA Ethics Code** and **Specialty Guidelines for Forensic Psychology**. The denial appears based on regulatory convenience rather than professional standards analysis.

### **4. No Meaningful Explanation Provided**

Mr. Nuno received **no substantive explanation** for the complaint denial, preventing meaningful appeal or correction. This violates due process requirements under Washington's **Uniform Disciplinary Act** (RCW 18.130).

# Institutional Reform Implications

## Forensic Psychology Regulation Reform

The Miranda case demonstrates **systemic failures** in forensic psychology oversight requiring:

### **1. Mandatory Recording Requirements**

All forensic evaluations should be **audio/video recorded** to prevent fabrication of patient statements and enable meaningful regulatory review.

### **2. Conflict of Interest Disclosure**

Forensic evaluators must disclose **all institutional relationships** that could bias their conclusions, particularly connections to law enforcement or prosecutorial agencies.

### **3. Independent Review Panels**

Complaints against forensic evaluators should be reviewed by **independent panels** including defense attorneys, civil rights advocates, and consumer representatives - not just psychology professionals.

### **4. Enhanced Investigation Standards**

Regulatory investigations must include **direct evidence review** (audio recordings, witness interviews) rather than relying solely on written submissions.

## Court System Accountability

Courts utilizing forensic evaluations must implement:

### **1. Judicial Education**

Judges need training on **forensic psychology limitations** and potential for bias/misconduct in court-ordered evaluations.

### **2. Alternative Evaluator Networks**

Courts must maintain **multiple qualified evaluators** to prevent monopolistic practices and enable meaningful choice.

### **3. Evaluation Quality Standards**

Courts should require **specific formatting and evidentiary standards** for forensic evaluations, including disclosure of all source materials and methodology.

### **4. Post-Evaluation Review**

Courts should implement **systematic review** of forensic evaluations for quality and accuracy, particularly in cases with adverse outcomes.

## Professional Accountability Measures

The psychology profession must address:

### **1. Ethics Code Enhancement**

APA Ethics Code needs **specific provisions** for forensic psychology practice, including stricter standards for court-ordered evaluations.

### **2. Professional Education Requirements**

Forensic psychologists should complete **mandatory continuing education** in ethics, bias recognition, and legal standards.

### **3. Peer Review Systems**

Professional organizations should implement **mandatory peer review** for forensic evaluations, particularly in high-stakes criminal cases.

### **4. Whistleblower Protection**

Psychology professionals reporting colleague misconduct need **protection from retaliation** and professional ostracism.
