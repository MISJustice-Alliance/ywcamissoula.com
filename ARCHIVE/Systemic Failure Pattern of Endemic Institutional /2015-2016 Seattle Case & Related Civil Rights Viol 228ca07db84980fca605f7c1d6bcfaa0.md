## Introduction

Seattle Municipal Court case 613225 presents compelling evidence of malicious prosecution, procedural violations, and civil rights deprivations under both Washington state law and federal constitutional protections. The case, filed against Elvis Nuno based on false reports from Danielle Chard, was **dismissed with prejudice due to complete absence of evidence**[1]. The circumstances surrounding this case reveal systematic procedural failures, ethical violations by Seattle police officers, and clear instances of malicious prosecution that warrant comprehensive legal analysis.

## Case 613225: Dismissal Analysis

### Dismissal Order Details

The Seattle Municipal Court's Order of Dismissal in case 613225 demonstrates a clear finding of prosecutorial failure[1]. The court marked "Dismissed with prejudice" and selected "Proof" as the reason code (MCIS reason code PRF), indicating a complete lack of evidence to support the charges[1]. Notably, the order also terminated the "No contact Order for Danielle chard," further supporting the conclusion that the underlying allegations were baseless[1].

### Legal Significance of Dismissal With Prejudice

Under Washington law, a dismissal with prejudice indicates that the prosecution was so fundamentally flawed that refiling the charges would be inappropriate[2]. This finding, combined with the specific notation of lack of proof, establishes a strong foundation for malicious prosecution claims under RCW 9.62.010, which criminalizes causing "another to be arrested or proceeded against for any crime of which he or she is innocent" when done "maliciously and without probable cause"[2][3].

## Elements of Malicious Prosecution

### Washington State Law Analysis

Under RCW 9.62.010, malicious prosecution requires proof of:

1. **Malicious intent** - acting with improper motive rather than bringing someone to justice[4]
2. **Absence of probable cause** - lack of reasonable basis for prosecution[5]
3. **Prosecution of an innocent person** - ultimate dismissal with prejudice demonstrates innocence[2]

The documentation reveals that all three elements are satisfied in case 613225.

### Evidence of Malicious Intent

The correspondence with Seattle's Office for Professional Accountability (OPA) reveals clear evidence of malicious intent[6][7][8]. In his complaint 2016OPA-1167, Nuno documented that:

- The original Edmonds Police report (2016-00009660) specifically stated that "Nuno did not attempt to contact Chard or have another party contact her on his behalf, there was no violation of the order"[7][8]
- Seattle Police Officer Erik Eastgard wrote a contradictory report claiming Chard "received an e-mail sent directly from me, in violation of the Order of Protection"[9][10]
- No email was ever produced or investigated, despite this being the central allegation[7][8]

### Absence of Probable Cause

Officer Guzley's initial report in case 613225 acknowledged that "None of the messages she provided contain direct threats"[11][12][13]. The officer used Chard's phone to make an improper contact with Nuno, which violated standard police procedures[14][15]. Multiple law enforcement sources confirmed that "no officer would make such a call on a civilian's phone" and that proper procedure required official paperwork and in-person notification[14][15].

## Danielle Chard's False Reports: Pattern of Defamation

### Documented False Statements

Nuno's comprehensive written statement to Seattle PD incident 2016-348587 details systematic false reporting by Chard[14][15]:

1. **Alaska Court Order Fabrication**: Chard claimed she attempted to obtain a court order in Alaska, but Nuno had never lived in Alaska and no such proceedings ever occurred[14][15]
2. **Technology Capabilities**: Chard alleged Nuno could "bypass the security block on her phone," despite IT security not being his specialty and such bypassing being technologically impossible[14][15]
3. **Contact Timeline**: Chard claimed no contact since May 2015, but documented evidence shows they met in October 2015[14][15]
4. **Phone Records Contradiction**: Chard's own phone records showed she routinely called Nuno, contradicting her claims of blocking him[14][15]

### Pattern of Strategic False Reporting

The evidence demonstrates that Chard's false reports escalated systematically:

- Initial harassment report in December 2015 (GO2015-421345)[11][12][13]
- Protection order violation allegations in April 2016 (GO2016-148053)[9][10]
- Each subsequent report contained increasingly serious false allegations

## Procedural Ethical Violations and OPA Complaint 2016OPA-1167

### Core Violations Documented

The OPA complaint reveals multiple procedural failures[6][16][7][8]:

1. **Report Falsification**: A police report was "ENTIRELY RE-WRITTEN to state the absolute direct opposite" of the original Edmonds report without any new evidence[6][16]
2. **Failure to Investigate**: No attempt was made to obtain or verify the alleged email that formed the basis of the charges[7][8]
3. **Excessive Bail**: $75,000 bail was set for charges that ultimately had no evidence, causing two weeks of unlawful incarceration[6][16][7][8]

### OPA Response and Tonia Winchester Correspondence

Deputy Director Tonia Winchester's response to the complaint demonstrates institutional indifference to documented violations[7][8]. Despite clear evidence of report falsification and procedural failures, OPA dismissed the complaint, stating that officers "were operating from the information provided by the subject of the complaint"[7][8]. This response ignores the fundamental duty to investigate and verify allegations before proceeding with arrests.

Winchester's assertion that "SPD officers did not alter another agency's report" directly contradicts the documented evidence showing substantial changes between the Edmonds and Seattle reports[7][8]. The OPA's refusal to investigate these discrepancies constitutes a failure in the oversight system designed to ensure police accountability.

## Civil Rights Violations Under Federal Law

### 42 U.S.C. § 1983 Claims

The pattern of conduct establishes strong federal civil rights claims[17][18][19][20][21]:

1. **Fourth Amendment Violations**: False arrest without probable cause constitutes unreasonable seizure[22][20]
2. **Fourteenth Amendment Due Process**: Prosecution based on known false information violates due process rights[21]
3. **Color of State Law**: All violations occurred while officers were acting in their official capacity[20][21]

### Qualified Immunity Analysis

Officers lose qualified immunity protection when they "knowingly filed a false affidavit to secure an arrest warrant" or when they "knowingly and deliberately, or with a reckless disregard for the truth, made false statements"[21]. The documented discrepancies between the Edmonds and Seattle reports establish that officers acted with at least reckless disregard for the truth.

## Professional Malpractice: Patricia Fulton

### Legal Malpractice Analysis

The Declaration of Ineffective Assistance documents Patricia Fulton's failures in representing Nuno[23][24]:

1. **Failure to Interview Key Witnesses**: Fulton refused to interview Danielle Chard or Elise Hall, despite their relevance to the defense[23][24]
2. **Failure to Obtain Crucial Evidence**: Despite repeated requests, Fulton failed to obtain the Seattle police reports that would have revealed the contradictions[23][24]
3. **Coercive Plea Negotiations**: Fulton presented a false choice between guilty plea and jail, without adequate preparation for defense[23][24]

### Comparison to Adequate Representation

The declaration notes that Raymond Delos Reyes, the public defender in Seattle case 613225, did interview Chard, spoke with Nuno's therapist, and obtained the prosecutor's investigation records[23][24]. This proper investigation led to the dismissal of the Seattle charges for lack of proof[23][24]. Fulton's failure to conduct similar investigation constitutes clear malpractice.

## Legal Precedent and Statutory Framework

### Washington State Malicious Prosecution

Under the established elements for malicious prosecution in Washington[26]:

1. **Civil claim was made** - Multiple charges filed in case 613225
2. **No probable cause** - Dismissal with prejudice for lack of proof
3. **Motivated by malice** - Pattern of false reporting and procedural violations
4. **Failed to prove claim** - Complete dismissal of all charges
5. **Caused injury** - Documented economic and psychological harm

### Federal Civil Rights Framework

The violations satisfy both elements of § 1983 claims[22]:

1. **Acting under color of state law** - All defendants were government actors
2. **Deprivation of constitutional rights** - Fourth and Fourteenth Amendment violations clearly established
