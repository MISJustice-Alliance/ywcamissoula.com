# 2016 Seattle False Police Report & Constitutional Violations

## Case Summary

The incident documented in Seattle OPA Case 2016OPA-1167 presents a clear pattern of constitutional violations involving police report fabrication, unlawful detention, excessive bail, and systemic oversight failures. The documentary evidence reveals that a police report fundamentally changed as it passed through three law enforcement agencies—Edmonds PD, King County Sheriff's Office, and Seattle PD—transforming from "no evidence of contact" to an allegation of direct email communication that never occurred. This fabricated evidence directly lead to an arrest, detention for two weeks, and $75,000 bail on non-violent misdemeanor charges, all without any supporting evidence. The subsequent dismissal of Seattle Municipal Court Case #613225 confirms the absence of any actual violation.

## Factual Timeline & Document Analysis

### **Originating Report (Edmonds PD)**

Edmonds Police Officer Samuel Gagner (#2899) in Report #2016-00009660 explicitly documented: "I confirmed that there is an active order of protection in place between Chard and Nuno, however, since Nuno did not attempt to contact Chard or have another party contact her on his behalf, there was no violation of the order." Officer Gagner further noted he was documenting Chard's statement "for further evidence of Chard's erratic behavior for future court proceedings."

### **Report Transmission & Alteration**

The report was faxed from Edmonds PD to King County Sheriff's Department, then from King County Sheriff's to Seattle PD. During this inter-jurisdictional transmission, the fundamental narrative changed from documentation of no violation to an allegation that Mr Nuno sent an email to Chard in violation of the protection order.

### **Seattle PD Report (GO#2016-148053)**

The Seattle report claimed Chard received "an e-mail sent directly from [Mr Nuno], in violation of the Order of Protection." Critically, no email was ever: (1) captured or preserved as evidence, (2) attached to the police report, (3) investigated or verified, or (4) produced during criminal proceedings.

### **Judicial Proceedings**

Despite attorney Raymond Delos Reyes pointing out that no email was attached to the police report, the presiding judge stated "I don't see why a police officer would lie" and set bail at $75,000—an amount grossly excessive for alleged non-violent misdemeanor charges of cyberstalking and harassment. Mr Nuno spent two weeks in Seattle Municipal Jail while Mr Nuno’s family raised $7,500 (10% of bail). All charges in Case #613225 were ultimately dismissed due to complete absence of evidence.

# Constitutional Violations Analysis

## Fourth Amendment: False Arrest & Unlawful Detention

### **Elements of Fourth Amendment Violation Under 42 U.S.C. § 1983:**

1. **Arrest/Detention Without Probable Cause**: Probable cause exists when officers have "facts and circumstances within their knowledge sufficient to warrant a reasonable belief that the suspect had committed or was committing a crime." The existence of probable cause at the time of arrest constitutes an absolute bar to a § 1983 false arrest claim.

In this case, the Seattle PD report fabricated the sole basis for probable cause—the alleged email. The originating Edmonds report explicitly documented no violation occurred. The absence of any actual email, combined with the officer's failure to investigate or verify the allegation before arrest, demonstrates clear lack of probable cause.

1. **Continuing Unlawful Detention**: Pretrial detention extended for two weeks without legitimate probable cause. Under *Manuel v. City of Joliet*, claims for unlawful pretrial detention are governed by the Fourth Amendment, and such claims accrue when the detention occurs, not when charges are dismissed.
2. **Inter-Jurisdictional Coordination Creating Liability**: The transmission of the report through three agencies (Edmonds PD → King County Sheriff → Seattle PD) raises questions about each agency's role in the constitutional violation. The material alteration of the report during inter-agency transmission suggests either deliberate fabrication or gross negligence in report handling.

## Fourteenth Amendment: Due Process Violations Through Fabricated Evidence

### **Deliberate Fabrication of Evidence:**

Multiple federal circuits recognize that fabricated evidence violates due process rights regardless of conviction. As stated by the Ninth Circuit: "There is a clearly established constitutional due process right not to be subjected to criminal charges on the basis of false evidence that was deliberately fabricated by the government."

### **Elements of Fabrication Claim:**

1. **Deliberate Fabrication**: The transformation of Officer Gagner's explicit finding of "no violation" into a Seattle PD report alleging direct email contact constitutes deliberate fabrication.
2. **Deprivation of Liberty**: Mr Nuno suffered two weeks of detention and months of pretrial restrictions directly caused by the fabricated evidence.
3. **Corruption of Truth-Seeking Function**: The fabricated report corrupted judicial proceedings, causing the judge to rely on false information when setting bail and evaluating probable cause.

The due process violation is independent of the Fourth Amendment seizure claim. As courts have recognized, "pretrial detention caused by fabricated evidence implicates both the Fourth Amendment and Fourteenth Amendment."

## Eighth Amendment: Excessive Bail

### **Constitutional Standard:**

Bail is "excessive" under the Eighth Amendment when set "at a figure higher than an amount reasonably calculated" to ensure the defendant's appearance at trial or protect public safety.

### **Application to This Case:**

1. **$75,000 Bail for Non-Violent Misdemeanors**: The charges were cyberstalking and harassment—non-violent, low-level offenses. The bail amount was not reasonably calculated to ensure court appearance but rather appeared punitive.
2. **Comparison to Similar Offenses**: Bail of $75,000 vastly exceeds typical amounts for similar non-violent misdemeanor offenses, particularly where the defendant has community ties and no history of flight risk.
3. **Based on Fabricated Evidence**: The excessive bail was predicated entirely on the false police report, compounding the constitutional violation.

## Malicious Prosecution Elements

### **42 U.S.C. § 1983 Malicious Prosecution Claims:**

Common law elements adapted for § 1983 claims:

1. **Initiation of Prosecution Without Probable Cause**: Seattle PD initiated prosecution based on fabricated evidence with no actual probable cause.
2. **Favorable Termination**: Case #613225 was dismissed due to absence of evidence—a clear favorable termination.
3. **Deprivation of Liberty**: Mr Nuno suffered two weeks detention plus pretrial restrictions.
4. **Malice/Improper Purpose**: The deliberate fabrication of evidence demonstrates malice or purpose other than bringing Mr Nuno to justice.

## Qualified Immunity Challenges

### **Overcoming Qualified Immunity:**

Officers may claim qualified immunity unless they violated "clearly established" constitutional rights that every reasonable officer would have known were unlawful.

### **Why Qualified Immunity Should Fail Here:**

1. **Clearly Established Right**: By 2016, it was clearly established that fabricating evidence violates constitutional rights.
2. **No Reasonable Mistake**: No reasonable officer could believe that arresting someone based on an email that was never investigated, captured, or verified constitutes lawful conduct.
3. **Deliberate Fabrication vs. Reasonable Mistake**: Qualified immunity does not protect officers who "deliberately violate law, fabricate evidence, or falsify reports."

The transformation of "no violation" to "email sent" during inter-agency transmission demonstrates either deliberate fabrication or such reckless disregard for truth that qualified immunity should not apply.

## Seattle OPA Investigation Failures

### **Systemic Accountability Breakdown:**

The OPA investigation closure despite documentary evidence of report fabrication reveals systemic oversight failures:

1. **Failure to Obtain Originating Report**: OPA claimed inability to access the Edmonds report showing no violation occurred.
2. **Failure to Investigate Inter-Agency Transmission**: OPA did not examine how the report changed during transmission through King County Sheriff to Seattle PD.
3. **Improper Closure Without Investigation**: OPA closed the case without investigating the documentary evidence Mr Nuno provided showing report discrepancies.
4. **Policy Against Reopening**: The OPA investigator admitted he saw "serious impropriety" and "obvious evidence of a violation" but stated the closed complaint could not be reopened—a policy that defeats accountability.

This mirrors findings from the 2011 DOJ Investigation of Seattle Police Department, which found that "OPA's current classification and findings systems are so complex that they damage OPA's credibility and undermine public confidence."

## Statute of Limitations Analysis

### **Washington State § 1983 Claims:**

Because 42 U.S.C. § 1983 contains no specific statute of limitations, federal courts apply the forum state's statute of limitations for personal injury actions.

**Three-Year Limitations Period:**

In Washington State, the statute of limitations for § 1983 claims is three years, based on RCW 4.16.080's personal injury limitations period.

### **Accrual Date:**

1. **False Arrest/Unlawful Detention**: Claims accrue when the unlawful detention occurs or when the plaintiff is released from custody.
2. **Fabricated Evidence with Pretrial Detention**: Recent Supreme Court guidance in *McDonough v. Smith* (2019) indicates that fabricated evidence claims may not accrue until prosecution concludes, as the ongoing detention represents a continuing violation.

### **Status as of November 2025:**

- **Incident Date**: 2016
- **Case Dismissal**: Likely 2016 or early 2017
- **Standard SOL Expiration**: 2019-2020

**Critical Issue**: The statute of limitations appears to have expired for direct § 1983 claims against the officers and agencies involved. However, this creates a potential **legal malpractice claim** against attorneys who failed to timely pursue civil rights claims, particularly if not advised of the limitations period.

## Legal Malpractice Considerations

### **2016 Attorney Search:**

Mr Nuno described receiving responses from multiple attorneys that while Mr Nuno had a case, "the time and resources it would require to file a case against Seattle PD was far too large for any firm to undertake, and the chance of any recoup of the legal costs would be unlikely."

### **Potential Malpractice Issues:**

1. **Failure to Advise of SOL**: If attorneys failed to clearly advise of the three-year statute of limitations while evaluating the case, they may have committed malpractice by allowing claims to become time-barred. This is particularly applicable to the MT bar complaint, [ODC 25-147 Right to Review](MT%20Bar%20Complaint%20ODC-15-147%20-%20Right%20To%20Request%20Rev%202acca07db84980c29efffe5373634c28.md).

## Institutional & Systemic Patterns

### **Inter-Jurisdictional Coordination Concerns:**

The transmission of reports through three separate agencies (Edmonds → King County → Seattle) without accountability for material alterations reveals systemic problems in inter-agency information sharing:

1. **Lack of Chain of Custody**: No documentation of who altered the report during transmission
2. **Absence of Verification Protocols**: Seattle PD relied on transmitted report without independent investigation
3. **Jurisdictional Coordination Violations**: Unauthorized or improper information sharing between jurisdictions

### **Pattern Practice Implications:**

This incident occurred during the period when Seattle PD was under DOJ investigation and subsequent consent decree (initiated 2012) for pattern-or-practice violations. The OPA's failure to properly investigate this complaint exemplifies the systemic accountability failures the DOJ identified.
