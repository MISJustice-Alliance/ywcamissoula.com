## I. Grounds for Complaint

I allege that Bryan Tipp, while representing me in criminal proceedings (State v. Nuno, Missoula County), committed legal malpractice and violated the Montana Rules of Professional Conduct by:

1. **Failure to Pursue Timely Civil Claims**: Despite clear evidence of civil rights violations (retaliatory prosecution, defamation, and harassment by Missoula PD/YWCA), Mr. Tipp advised against filing civil claims to "give the matter time," directly causing the expiration of statutes of limitations for:
    - **42 U.S.C. § 1983 claims** (3-year statute under *Wallace v. Kato*).
    - **Defamation claims** (3-year statute under MCA § 27-2-204).
    - **Intentional Infliction of Emotional Distress** (3-year statute under MCA § 27-2-204).
2. **Conflict of Interest**: Prioritizing the criminal defense’s expediency over civil remedies aligned with prosecutorial interests, violating Rule 1.7(a)(2) MRPC (conflict between client/lawyer responsibilities)[1][2].
3. **Lack of Diligence**: Failing to investigate civil options despite documented evidence (e.g., dismissed charges, SWAT incident, YWCA harassment), breaching Rule 1.3 MRPC[1][2].

# II. Factual Background

- **2018–2020 Representation**: Mr. Tipp defended me against misdemeanor/felony stalking charges (dismissed with prejudice in 2018/2019)[3][4][5].
- **Explicit Advice**: He discouraged civil action, stating, "give the matter time," while aware of:
    - Ongoing harassment by Officer Ethan Smith and YWCA affiliates[3][5].
    - Career losses exceeding $1.5M due to defamation[3][4].
- **Statute Expiration**: Key deadlines (e.g., 2015 Edmonds SWAT incident, 2017 retaliatory arrests) lapsed by 2020 without civil filings[3][6].

# III. Legal and Ethical Violations

# **Expanded Malpractice for Failure to Address Ongoing Harassment and Economic Damages**

## I. Supplemental Allegations

Bryan Tipp’s malpractice extended beyond initial statutory limitations failures to include **willful disregard of documented ongoing harassment** (May 2020–August 2022) targeting Mr. Nuno’s business, *Pacific NW Rural Broadband Alliance*. Despite receiving evidence of:

1. **Continued Defamation Campaign**: YWCA associates’ false allegations to the St. Regis School District (client) that Mr. Nuno posed "public safety risks," directly causing termination of a $60,000/year contract in June 2024 .
2. **Business Interference**: Harassment included spoofed calls to clients (e.g., "Elvis is under federal investigation") and fraudulent online reviews .
3. **Direct Financial Impact**: Loss of the St. Regis contract compounded prior career losses, totaling $2.16M in cumulative damages .

Tipp’s inaction violated:

- **Rule 1.4 MRPC** (Communication): Failed to inform Mr. Nuno of civil remedies for business torts (e.g., tortious interference under MCA § 27-1-712).
- **Rule 1.3 MRPC** (Diligence): Ignored 17 documented harassment incidents submitted to his office between 2020–2022 .

## II. Evidence of Tipp’s Knowledge

- **Client Correspondence**: May 2019–August 2022, attaching YWCA-linked social media posts defaming *Pacific NW Rural Broadband Alliance* .
- **Contract Termination Notice**: St. Regis School District’s June 30, 2024, following slander  citing "community safety concerns" instigated by YWCA associates .
- **Tipp’s Response**: Handwritten note on August 4, 2021, client file: "No civil action—too speculative" despite documented causation .

## III. Legal Consequences

Tipp’s omissions directly enabled:

1. **Additional Statute Expirations**: Missed deadlines for:
    - **Tortious Interference Claims** (3-year statute under MCA § 27-2-204)
    - **Montana Deceptive Trade Practices Act** claims (MCA § 30-14-133)
2. **Compounding Damages**: Loss of the St. Regis contract triggered collapse of two subcontracts worth $120,000 annually .

## IV. Requested Disciplinary Action

Pursuant to MCA § 37-61-406, the Office of Disciplinary Counsel should:

1. **Expand Investigation** to include Tipp’s failure to address post-2019-2022 business torts.
2. **Require Restitution** for the St. Regis contract loss ($180,000 over 3 years).
3. **Refer for Sanctions** under Rule 6.1 MRPC (Competence) and Rule 1.16 (Declining Representation When Unprepared).

**Attachments Added**:

This supplement establishes a pattern of **deliberate indifference to ongoing civil rights violations and economic sabotage**, warranting enhanced disciplinary measures.

## V. Post Script: Personal Addendum

Beyond the formal legal and technical analysis of Bryan Tipp’s conduct during and after the conclusion of my case, I have developed serious concerns based on nearly three years of direct observation. It appears that Mr. Tipp has, at times, struggled to maintain a clear and consistent distinction between advocating in the best interest of his clients and aligning, perhaps too closely, with the interests of the prosecution. His actions suggest a strong preference for maintaining institutional harmony, often to the point of avoiding any course of action that might challenge the status quo—particularly when it involves organizations viewed as having elevated or unquestionable authority.

In my letter to the YWCA, which preceded a substantial legal escalation against me, I emphasized that their mission is fundamentally important. However, in this and other cases I have personally observed, there appears to be a troubling pattern: abuses of power, questionable prosecutorial conduct, significant civil rights concerns, and a tendency to defend or shield individuals accused of domestic or child abuse—when those individuals are women.

The close and, at times, opaque relationship between the YWCA, the Missoula Police Department, and the Missoula City Prosecutor’s Office raises serious questions about institutional accountability. In my case, Mr. Tipp’s approach and decisions arguably contributed to a lack of scrutiny of these entities, allowing conduct that I believe to be harmful—and potentially unlawful—to persist without challenge or transparency.
