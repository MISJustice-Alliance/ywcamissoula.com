# MT Bar Complaint ODC No. 25-147:  ODC Ruling (November 2025)

Owner: MISJustice Alliance
Verification: Verified
Tags: Legal Malpractice, MT Bar Complaint, Montana, ODC-25-147, Official Complaint

<aside>
💡

The Office of Disciplinary Counsel (ODC) for the State of Montana has reviewed and dismissed a grievance filed by Elvis Nuno against his former attorney, Bryan C. Tipp, concerning Tipp’s representation in a series of criminal matters from 2018 to 2021. Nuno’s complaint alleges Tipp violated several Montana Rules of Professional Conduct by failing to pursue timely civil rights claims related to police and YWCA-affiliated harassment, which led to lost recovery due to expired statutes of limitations, and by prioritizing the prosecution’s interests over his client’s. Tipp’s defense, supported by fee agreements and correspondence, was that he was only retained for criminal defense—not civil litigation—and repeatedly advised Nuno to seek separate counsel for civil matters. The ODC found that Tipp fulfilled his obligations by achieving dismissal of all criminal charges and provided sufficient communication and competent representation within the agreed scope. Concluding there was no clear evidence of ethical violations by Tipp under Rules 1.1, 1.2, 1.3, or 1.4, the ODC dismissed the grievance but informed Nuno of his right to seek further review by the Commission on Practice within thirty days.

</aside>
