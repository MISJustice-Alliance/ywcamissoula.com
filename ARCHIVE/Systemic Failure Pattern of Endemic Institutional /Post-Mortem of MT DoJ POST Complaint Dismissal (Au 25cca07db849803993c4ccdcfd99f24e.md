# The Fundamental Misunderstanding

The POST dismissal of Mr. Nuno's complaint in less than 24 hours represents a profound failure to comprehend the nature and scope of the allegations presented or follow through with the most rudimentary due diligence in reviewing the materials submitted. The response stating that "POST does not investigate allegations that are over 5 years old" fundamentally mischaracterizes what the documentation clearly establishes: **this is not a case of isolated historical incidents, but rather an ongoing pattern of institutional harassment perpetrated in large part by Montana law enforcement agencies that continues to the present day**.

# Evidence of Ongoing Pattern Through 2025

The documents provide extensive evidence that the harassment campaign remains active:

## Recent Professional Sabotage (2024-2025)

- **June 2024**: St. Regis School District contract ($60,000/year) terminated after YWCA associates spread false allegations about Mr. Nuno posing "public safety risks"
- **2025**: Continued interference with employment opportunities through manipulation of background check systems
- **Daily harassment continuing**: "Regular threats via phone calls, social media, and personal interactions" documented through July 2025

## Current Law Enforcement Misconduct

- **Ongoing refusal to investigate**: Missoula Police Department continues to refuse harassment complaints and threatens Mr. Nuno when he attempts to report crimes
- **Active coordination**: Evidence shows continued coordination between YWCA employees and law enforcement to suppress complaints and protection from criminal investigation regarding the activities of YWCA employees
- **Technological harassment**: Ongoing spoofed calls, anonymous social media accounts, and cyberstalking campaigns with message content clearly tying these individuals to association with the YWCA.

# Evidence of Pattern Affecting Other Victims

The complaint is not about isolated incidents affecting only Mr. Nuno. The documentation reveals:

## Primary Named Victims

1. **Elvis Nuno** - an ongoing, decade-long pattern of coordinated civil rights violations, malicious prosecution, professional and economic sabotage, and institutional corruption across multiple jurisdictions carried out by law enforcement, prosecutors, and YWCA Missoula personnel, resulting in continuous personal, professional, and constitutional harm through 2025.
2. **Arthur Brown** - Public account describes "YWCA staff writing letters to judges recommending permanent restraining orders against him, despite their extremely limited knowledge of the parties" including providing advocacy for a woman with established criminal history involving child abuse, drug use, repeated malicious harassment of numerous individuals, and child endangerment leading to the loss of custody of multiple children from different fathers. 

## Systemic Pattern Evidence

- Mr. Nuno received contact from "families who read my google post and reached out to me describing similar such malicious and deliberately dishonest targeting by YWCA operations manager E’Lise Chard"
- Employee reviews spanning over a decade describe organizational dysfunction and "racist undertones"
- Pattern extends from at least 2012 (when Missoula was labeled the "rape capital of the country") continuing unabated through to 2025

## The Detective Brueckner Conflict

A critical revelation discovered in 2025: **Detective Connie Brueckner, who led the investigation against Mr. Nuno, simultaneously served on the YWCA Board of Directors**.

- **Egregious conflict of interest**: A law enforcement officer investigating complaints against employees of an organization where she held fiduciary duties
- **Institutional capture**: Law enforcement serving organizational interests rather than constitutional protections
- **Pattern enabler**: This structural conflict explains how law enforcement effectively ratifies criminally malicious behavior, refusing to take any action to provide necessary protections from the harassment campaign, allowing it to continue unimpeded

# Why This Matters for POST's Jurisdiction

The ongoing nature of these violations brings them squarely within POST's mandate:

1. **Current misconduct**: Officers are presently refusing to investigate crimes and threatening citizens
2. **Continuing violations doctrine**: The harassment represents an unbroken pattern, not isolated past events
3. **Public safety threat**: The pattern affects multiple community members and continues to present day
4. **Institutional failure**: The Brueckner conflict reveals systemic issues requiring immediate intervention

# Conclusion

POST's dismissal after less than one business day of review demonstrates either:

- Failure to actually review the extensive documentation provided
- Institutional unwillingness to address systemic corruption
- Fundamental misunderstanding of continuing violation principles

The evidence clearly establishes that this is not about "allegations that are over 5 years old" but about **endemic institutional corruption, broken policies, and fundamental lack of basic professional standards or accountability that started over a decade ago and continues to victimize Montana citizens today**. The discovery of Detective Brueckner's YWCA board membership while investigating YWCA complaints represents institutional corruption requiring immediate intervention, not dismissal based on a misapplied temporal limitation.

This pattern of immediate dismissal without proper review, combined with the mischaracterization of ongoing conduct as "old" violations, suggests potential administrative misconduct that warrants investigation by multiple oversight bodies. POST's statutory duty to investigate credible complaints of ongoing misconduct cannot be circumvented through arbitrary time limitations when the conduct continues.
