# Wrongful Termination - ADA Violation: Mr Nuno v. LoudFeed (July, 2025)

Owner: Elvis Nuno, MISJustice Alliance
Verification: Verified
Tags: Civil Rights Violations, Legal Analysis, Michigan, Ongoing Damages, Wrongful Termination

# Initial Summary

This witness statement presents multiple potential legal claims arising from what appears to be a deliberate and discriminatory termination of Mr Nuno following his disclosure of mental health conditions. The most significant claims include constructive discharge, ADA discrimination and retaliation, intentional infliction of emotional distress, and breach of implied employment contract. The facts strongly suggest that Ron Suarez manufactured a hostile situation to force Mr Nuno’s resignation after learning about his mental health conditions and trauma history.

# I. Factual Pattern

## Key Facts:

- **Unpaid Labor**: Mr Nuno performed over $10,000 worth of professional work without compensation[1](https://www.ssrn.com/abstract=3402040)
- **Housing as Primary Compensation**: Initial agreement was housing-for-work with future salary[1](https://www.ssrn.com/abstract=3402040)
- **"Our Home" Representation**: Ron referred to the property as "our home" — implying decided tenancy of Mr Nuno in Mr Suarez’s home[1](https://www.ssrn.com/abstract=3402040)
- **Forced Departure During Medical Crisis**: Mr Nuno explicitly stated "I am feeling threatened"[1](https://www.ssrn.com/abstract=3402040)
- **Multiple Intimidators**: Ron brought multiple people to confront Mr Nuno during panic attack[1](https://www.ssrn.com/abstract=3402040)
- **Stranded Without Support**: Mr Nuno was left stranded in unfamiliar city, forcing expensive emergency travel[1](https://www.ssrn.com/abstract=3402040)
- **Father's Damages**: Mr Nuno's disabled father also suffered losses from aborted relocation[1](https://www.ssrn.com/abstract=3402040)
- **Ron's Bad Faith**: Ron claimed to feel “very scared” and "vulnerable" by Mr Nuno’s reaction to his question (i.e. refusing to answer the question regarding legal matters, informing him the information had been provided in writing, and communicating a medical emergency that required him to leave the room) despite orchestrating the confrontation[1](https://www.ssrn.com/abstract=3402040)

# II. Wage and Hour Claims

## A. Quantum Meruit / Unjust Enrichment

Under Michigan law, quantum meruit applies when valuable services are provided with the expectation of compensation[1](https://www.ssrn.com/abstract=3402040)[2](https://www.mcser.org/journal/index.php/mjss/article/download/8768/8425)[3](https://www.ccsenet.org/journal/index.php/jpl/article/download/68642/37322). Mr Nuno has an exceptionally strong claim:

### **Elements Satisfied:**

1. **Valuable Services Provided**: Over $10,000 worth of work including business plans, team leadership, and infrastructure improvements[1](https://www.ssrn.com/abstract=3402040)
2. **Services Accepted**: Ron utilized Mr Nuno's work product and expertise[1](https://www.ssrn.com/abstract=3402040)
3. **Expectation of Payment**: Clear understanding of $130,000/year compensation[1](https://www.ssrn.com/abstract=3402040)
4. **Unjust Enrichment**: Ron received substantial benefit without paying anything[4](https://brill.com/downloadpdf/journals/jwit/22/2/article-p249_4.pdf)[5](http://www.scielo.cl/pdf/rdpucv/n33/a05.pdf)

Michigan courts recognize quantum meruit claims alongside employment claims[4](https://brill.com/downloadpdf/journals/jwit/22/2/article-p249_4.pdf)[5](http://www.scielo.cl/pdf/rdpucv/n33/a05.pdf). The fact that Mr Nuno was promised future compensation strengthens this claim significantly[3](https://www.ccsenet.org/journal/index.php/jpl/article/download/68642/37322)[4](https://brill.com/downloadpdf/journals/jwit/22/2/article-p249_4.pdf).

## B. Michigan Payment of Wages and Fringe Benefits Act

Mr Nuno may file a complaint with Michigan's Wage and Hour Division for unpaid wages[6](https://www.crimrxiv.com/pub/mlhqrc89/download/pdf). The Act provides for:

- Recovery of unpaid wages
- 10% penalty on unpaid amounts
- Potential exemplary damages
- Attorney fees and costs[7](https://www.michigan.gov/helpinghand/unemployment/filing-a-complaint-for-non-payment-of-wages-or-fringe-benefits)

# III. Constructive Discharge

The facts elevate this to an extreme case of constructive discharge[8](https://www.courts.michigan.gov/4afffe/siteassets/publications/impact/written/civil/impact-e-mail-12-27-22-civil.pdf):

## Aggravating Factors:

1. **Medical Emergency Disregard**: Ron ignored Mr Nuno's explicit statement of medical emergency[8](https://www.courts.michigan.gov/4afffe/siteassets/publications/impact/written/civil/impact-e-mail-12-27-22-civil.pdf)
2. **Escalating Intimidation**: Bringing multiple strangers to confront someone in crisis[9](https://legislature.mi.gov/Laws/MCL?objectName=mcl-408-488)
3. **Forced to State Fear**: Mr Nuno had to explicitly say "I am feeling threatened"[1](https://www.ssrn.com/abstract=3402040)
4. **Immediate Housing Loss**: Coupling job loss with immediate homelessness[10](https://law.justia.com/cases/federal/district-courts/FSupp/760/617/1421246/)[11](https://www.rigganlawfirm.com/blog/missouri-common-law-wage-claims-have-five-year-s/)

**Michigan law recognizes that forcing an employee experiencing a medical emergency to choose between their health and employment constitutes constructive discharge**[8](https://www.courts.michigan.gov/4afffe/siteassets/publications/impact/written/civil/impact-e-mail-12-27-22-civil.pdf)[9](https://legislature.mi.gov/Laws/MCL?objectName=mcl-408-488).

# IV. Severe ADA Violations

## A. Failure to Accommodate Medical Emergency

The ADA requires employers to accommodate employees experiencing disability-related medical emergencies[12](https://www.jacksonlewis.com/insights/guidance-ada-accommodations-and-medical-restrictions-plain-meaning-federal-appeals-court)[13](https://www.legislature.mi.gov/documents/1995-1996/billanalysis/Senate/htm/1995-SFA-1158-A.htm). Ron's actions represent an extreme violation:

1. **Ignored Medical Emergency Declaration**: Mr Nuno clearly stated he was having a medical issue[1](https://www.ssrn.com/abstract=3402040)
2. **Created Hostile Environment During Crisis**: Brought multiple strangers to intimidate and force confrontation[1](https://www.ssrn.com/abstract=3402040)
3. **Forced Departure**: Made environment so hostile Mr Nuno had to flee[14](https://mslawllp.com/the-impact-of-a-hostile-work-environment-on-disability-benefits-claims/)[15](https://www.justiceatwork.com/what-damages-can-you-receive-in-a-wrongful-termination-case/)

## B. Retaliation - Textbook Case

The timeline is damning:

- **July 24**: Mental health disclosure[1](https://www.ssrn.com/abstract=3402040)
- **July 26**: Orchestrated confrontation and termination[1](https://www.ssrn.com/abstract=3402040)

This 48-hour window between protected activity and adverse action creates an almost irrefutable inference of retaliation[16](https://oilfieldovertime.com/quantum-meruit-and-unjust-enrichment-in-flsa-cases/)[17](https://adata.org/factsheet/work-leave).

# V. Housing-Related Claims

## A. Illegal Eviction

Michigan law prohibits immediate eviction without proper notice, even when housing is tied to employment[18](https://mlpp.org/new-report-underscores-critical-need-for-swift-legislation-to-protect-michigan-families-harmed-by-eviction/)[19](https://panor.ru/articles/otpusk-meditsinskikh-rabotnikov-dolzhen-byt-obespechen-rabotodatelem-na-zametku-bukhgalteru/90873.html). Required elements:

1. **Written Notice**: None provided[20](https://www.semanticscholar.org/paper/4a684cacea859c75b7606782fe3a017ee4b7d514)
2. **Opportunity to Cure**: None given[20](https://www.semanticscholar.org/paper/4a684cacea859c75b7606782fe3a017ee4b7d514)
3. **Due Process**: Completely absent[18](https://mlpp.org/new-report-underscores-critical-need-for-swift-legislation-to-protect-michigan-families-harmed-by-eviction/)

Mr Nuno was effectively subjected to an illegal "self-help" eviction during a medical emergency[21](https://npissh.ro/roxana-cristina-radu-new-forms-of-leave-and-allowances-granted-to-employees-in-special-family-situations/).

## B. Breach of Housing Agreement

Ron's repeated statements that it was "our home" may create additional contractual obligations[1](https://www.ssrn.com/abstract=3402040). The immediate forced departure violates basic landlord-tenant protections[18](https://mlpp.org/new-report-underscores-critical-need-for-swift-legislation-to-protect-michigan-families-harmed-by-eviction/)[22](https://www.semanticscholar.org/paper/9f0e59a52ce18158c8e39c2262d6d7aa74983566).

# VI. Emotional Distress Claims

## Extreme and Outrageous Conduct:

2. **Physical Intimidation During Medical Crisis**: Multiple confronters during panic attack[1](https://www.ssrn.com/abstract=3402040)
3. **Gaslighting**: Claiming to be the “scared” and "vulnerable" party while attacking[1](https://www.ssrn.com/abstract=3402040)
4. **Abandonment**: Leaving Mr Nuno stranded in unfamiliar city[1](https://www.ssrn.com/abstract=3402040)

**This conduct transcends workplace disputes and enters the realm of deliberate cruelty**[23](https://www.mdpi.com/2076-393X/10/3/411).

## C. Punitive Damages

Ron's conduct appears malicious and calculated to cause maximum harm[25](https://www.semanticscholar.org/paper/c9b50da29a08f23a816f7079d4a537f1cc412493). Factors supporting punitive damages:

- Deliberate exploitation of disclosed mental health condition
- Physical intimidation during medical emergency
- Abandonment in unfamiliar city
- Complete disregard for employee welfare

# VIII. Criminal Law Considerations

## Potential Criminal Violations:

1. **False Imprisonment**: Blocking exit during medical emergency[26](https://www.dovepress.com/violence-toward-health-workers-in-bahrain-defense-force-royal-medical--peer-reviewed-article-OAEM)
    1. While Ron is a paraplegic he verbally attempted to block Mr Nuno’s exit by leveraging his authority as employer and landlord (recorded on camera). 
2. **Assault**: Creating reasonable fear of imminent harm[26](https://www.dovepress.com/violence-toward-health-workers-in-bahrain-defense-force-royal-medical--peer-reviewed-article-OAEM)
    1. Mr Nuno was unable to determine who was demanding entrance into his room, or what their intentions were, particularly as Mr Suarez was aware Mr Nuno was having a medical crisis.
3. **Theft of Services**: Accepting $10,000+ of work without payment and immediately blocking access to work systems to prevent Mr Nuno from retaining important evidentiary documentation. [27](https://www.semanticscholar.org/paper/01d6b13b36fd0ec9100bb6d180c0917c85a3e0da)

While criminal prosecution is discretionary, these facts may interest law enforcement.

# X. Conclusion

The facts demonstrate a strong case into one of the most egregious examples of disability discrimination, constructive discharge, and employer misconduct imaginable. Ron Suarez exploited Mr Nuno's vulnerability, extracted free labor, and then manufactured a crisis to force him out upon learning of his mental health conditions. The combination of unpaid wages, illegal eviction, discrimination, retaliation, and abandonment during a medical emergency creates overwhelming liability.

Mr Nuno should pursue all available remedies aggressively. The facts support not only substantial compensatory damages but also punitive damages given the malicious nature of Ron's conduct. This case exemplifies why robust enforcement of disability discrimination laws is essential to protect vulnerable workers from exploitation and abuse.
