# Real-World Questions This Content Answers

## **"How do I know if I have a valid civil rights claim in Montana?"**

Montana civil rights violations occur when someone discriminates against you based on protected characteristics in employment, housing, education, public accommodations, or government services[1](https://procterlawfirm.com/civil-rights-lawyer/)[3](https://erd.dli.mt.gov/human-rights/). Protected classes include:

- Race, color, national origin
- Age, sex, marital status
- Physical or mental disability
- Religion, creed, political beliefs
- Sexual orientation
- Retaliation for asserting rights

You must file with the Montana Human Rights Bureau within 180 days of the discriminatory act[1](https://procterlawfirm.com/civil-rights-lawyer/)[3](https://erd.dli.mt.gov/human-rights/).

## **"What constitutes prosecutorial misconduct?"**

Prosecutorial misconduct includes[2](https://www.fairfightinitiative.org/abuse-of-power-by-prosecutors/):

- **Offering false evidence** - Presenting evidence known to be inadmissible or false
- **Suppressing Brady evidence** - Hiding exculpatory evidence from the defense
- **Suborning perjury** - Encouraging witnesses to lie
- **Prosecutorial bluffing** - Making improper threats or misrepresentations

## **"How do I file a complaint against police misconduct in Montana?"**

For police misconduct:

1. Document everything immediately - dates, times, witnesses, injuries
2. File a complaint with the law enforcement agency's internal affairs
3. Contact the Montana Public Safety Officer Standards and Training Council (POST)[4](https://newstalkkgvo.com/former-sheriff-ibsen-egregious-misconduct-sustained-by-post/)[5](https://nbcmontana.com/news/local/state-council-says-evidence-proves-misconduct-by-former-missoula-co-sheriff)
4. File a civil rights complaint with Montana Human Rights Bureau within 180 days[3](https://erd.dli.mt.gov/human-rights/)
5. Consider filing a federal civil rights lawsuit under 42 U.S.C. § 1983
