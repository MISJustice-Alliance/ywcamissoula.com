### The Municipal Court's Denial

### **Legal Standards Applied**

The municipal court was required to apply the **"manifest injustice" standard** under **CrRLJ 4.2(f)**, which mandates that courts "shall allow a defendant to withdraw the defendant's plea of guilty whenever it appears that the withdrawal is necessary to correct a manifest injustice"[5].

### **Judicial Analysis Deficiencies**

The court's denial appears to have been based on several problematic factors:

1. **Incomplete Record**: Without defense counsel present, the court lacked a complete factual record to evaluate the ineffective assistance claim
2. **Presumption of Validity**: Courts typically presume plea colloquies were adequate absent clear evidence to the contrary[3]
3. **Burden of Proof**: The burden rested entirely on Mr Nuno to demonstrate both deficient performance and prejudice under **Strickland**[4]

### **Manifest Injustice Analysis**

Under Washington law, manifest injustice encompasses four primary categories[3]:

- Denial of effective assistance of counsel
- Involuntary plea (coerced or uninformed)
- Plea agreement not kept by the State
- Agreement not signed by either party

Mr Nuno’s case potentially fell within the first category, but the court's analysis was likely impaired by the absence of counsel’s ability to present the necessary evidence and legal arguments.
