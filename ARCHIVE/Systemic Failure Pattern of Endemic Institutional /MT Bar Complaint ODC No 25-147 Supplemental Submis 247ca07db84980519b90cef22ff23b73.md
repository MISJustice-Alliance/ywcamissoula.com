# MT Bar Complaint ODC No. 25-147: Supplemental Submission #1 (August 2025)

Owner: Elvis Nuno, MISJustice Alliance
Verification: Verified
Tags: Bryan Tipp, Legal Malpractice, MT Bar Complaint, Missoula, Montana

# I. INTRODUCTION

This supplemental submission provides additional documentation and analysis of attorney Bryan Tipp's professional misconduct in his representation of Elvis Nuno from 2018-2022. The evidence presented herein demonstrates multiple violations of the Montana Rules of Professional Conduct (MRPC), resulting in substantial prejudice to Mr. Nuno's constitutional rights and civil remedies.

# II. OVERVIEW OF ADDITIONAL VIOLATIONS

Mr. Tipp's representation was characterized by:

- Gross negligence in failing to identify critical conflicts of interest
- Complete failure to file any substantive motions
- Inadequate investigation of readily discoverable Brady material
- Failure to protect client's constitutional rights
- Ineffective assistance resulting in loss of civil claims

# III. DETAILED ANALYSIS OF PROFESSIONAL MISCONDUCT

## A. Violation of MRPC Rule 1.1 - Competence

### **1. Failure to Investigate Detective Brueckner's Conflict of Interest**

The most egregious failure in Mr. Tipp's representation was his complete failure to discover that Detective Brueckner, who was reassigned to Mr. Nuno's case after Officer Ethan Smith's removal, served on the Board of Directors of the YWCA of Missoula. This failure demonstrates:

- **Lack of Basic Due Diligence**: A simple public records search would have revealed Detective Brueckner's board position
- **Failure to Understand Case Context**: The entire prosecution centered on Mr. Nuno's criticism of the YWCA
- **Ignoring Red Flags**: Officer Smith was removed for "obvious appearance of impropriety" due to his relationship with E'Lise Chard, yet Mr. Tipp failed to investigate why the case was reassigned to Brueckner

### **2. Failure to Recognize Constitutional Violations**

Mr. Tipp failed to identify or address clear violations of Mr. Nuno's 14th Amendment rights:

- **Conscience-Shocking Behavior** under County of Sacramento v. Lewis, 523 U.S. 833 (1998):
    - Institutional conspiracy between multiple agencies
    - Detective with fiduciary duties investigating her own organization
    - Deliberate targeting of Mr. Nuno's livelihood
    - Retaliatory prosecution for protected speech
- **Procedural Due Process Violations** under Brady v. Maryland, 373 U.S. 83 (1963):
    - Material information withheld (Brueckner's YWCA board service)
    - Deliberate concealment of exculpatory information
    - Conflict-infected investigation undermining entire prosecution

## B. Violation of MRPC Rule 1.3 - Diligence

### **1. Complete Failure to File Motions**

Despite overwhelming evidence of misconduct, Mr. Tipp filed NO motions addressing:

- **Brady Violations**: Failed to file motions to compel disclosure of Detective Brueckner's conflicts
- **Motions to Dismiss**: Never challenged the prosecution despite clear constitutional violations
- **Motions to Disqualify**: Failed to seek disqualification of conflicted investigators/prosecutors
- **Suppression Motions**: Did not challenge illegally obtained evidence

### **2. Failure to Address Eighth Amendment Violations**

Mr. Tipp took no action regarding excessive bail amounts used as coercive mechanisms:

- $50,000, $75,000, and $100,000 bail for non-violent charges
- Pattern of dismissing and re-filing charges to re-arrest and increase bail
- No motion for bail reduction or challenge to unconstitutional bail practices

### **3. Failure to Challenge Pre-Trial Punishment**

GPS monitoring imposed severe restrictions without any showing of flight risk:

- Significant movement restrictions within Missoula
- Unable to leave county without permission
- Required regular reporting (effectively on parole while presumed innocent)
- Personal cost burden despite no evidence of flight risk
- Mr. Nuno was lifelong Missoula resident caring for elderly parents

## C. Violation of MRPC Rule 1.4 - Communication

### **1. Failure to Inform Client of Conflicts**

Mr. Tipp never informed Mr. Nuno about:

- Detective Brueckner's YWCA board position
- The significance of this conflict to the defense
- Available remedies and motions that could be filed

### **2. Inadequate Consultation on Strategy**

No evidence exists that Mr. Tipp:

- Discussed potential civil rights claims
- Explained statute of limitations implications
- Consulted about challenging the prosecution's obvious conflicts

## D. Violation of MRPC Rule 1.7 - Conflicts of Interest

While not directly conflicted himself, Mr. Tipp's failure to identify and address the prosecution's conflicts demonstrates fundamental misunderstanding of conflict principles and their importance to effective representation.

# IV. SPECIFIC INSTANCES OF MISCONDUCT

## A. The Transfer from Smith to Brueckner: Missed Opportunity

**Documentation shows:**

1. Officer Ethan Smith removed for "obvious appearance of impropriety"
2. Smith's commanding officer recognized conflict from "close personal relationship" with E'Lise Chard
3. Case transferred to Detective Brueckner
4. Brueckner had MORE SERIOUS conflict as YWCA board member
5. Mr. Tipp failed to investigate or discover this transfer's implications

**Required Actions Not Taken:**

- Motion to compel disclosure of reason for transfer
- Investigation into new detective's background
- Motion to disqualify based on institutional conflict

## B. Failure to Challenge Fabricated Allegations

**Evidence of Perjury and Fabrication:**

- E'Lise Chard claimed threats to kidnap her children (no evidence)
- Alleged danger to YWCA employees (no evidence)
- Claimed extensive criminal drug history (none exists)
- Admitted under oath only contact was 3 emails requesting cessation of harassment

**Mr. Tipp's Failures:**

- No motion for sanctions for perjury
- No criminal referral for false statements
- Failed to use admissions to seek dismissal

## C. Statute of Limitations Malpractice

**Lost Civil Rights Claims:**

- 42 U.S.C. § 1983 claims (3-year statute in Montana)
- Malicious prosecution claims
- False arrest/imprisonment claims
- Due process violations
- First Amendment retaliation claims

**Malpractice Elements:**

- Duty: Represent client competently including preserving civil remedies
- Breach: Failed to file any civil rights claims
- Causation: Direct result of inadequate representation
- Damages: Lost substantial civil remedies

# V. PATTERN OF INSTITUTIONAL MISCONDUCT IGNORED

## A. Coordinated Harassment Campaign

Mr. Tipp failed to recognize or address the pattern showing:

1. Initial charges filed after Mr. Nuno's complaint to YWCA
2. YWCA's involvement in pushing prosecution
3. Multiple agencies coordinating against Mr. Nuno
4. Use of criminal process to silence criticism

## B. Failure to Develop Theory of Defense

No evidence exists that Mr. Tipp ever developed coherent defense strategy addressing:

- First Amendment protected activity
- Retaliatory prosecution
- Institutional conflicts of interest
- Bad faith prosecution

# VI. COMPARISON TO PROFESSIONAL STANDARDS

## A. ABA Standards for Criminal Justice

Mr. Tipp's representation fell below every major standard:

- Failed to conduct adequate investigation
- Did not identify favorable evidence
- Ignored conflicts of interest
- Failed to protect constitutional rights

## B. Montana Public Defender Standards

Even measured against public defender standards (despite being retained counsel), Mr. Tipp's representation was deficient:

- No investigation of state's witnesses
- Failed to identify Brady material
- No challenge to unconstitutional practices

# VII. PREJUDICE TO CLIENT

## A. Criminal Case Prejudice

- Proceeded with conflict-infected investigation
- No challenges to tainted evidence
- Client convicted based on conflicted detective's work
- Constitutional violations unaddressed

## B. Civil Remedies Lost

- Statute of limitations expired on federal claims
- No recovery for malicious prosecution
- Lost compensation for constitutional violations
- Ongoing reputational harm

## C. Continued Harassment

- Pattern of harassment continued unabated
- No protective orders sought
- Client remained vulnerable to retaliation

# VIII. AGGRAVATING FACTORS

1. **Retained Counsel**: Mr. Tipp was privately paid, not an overworked public defender
2. **Clear Red Flags**: Officer removal for conflict should have triggered investigation
3. **Simple Discovery**: Detective's board service was public information
4. **Central to Case**: YWCA conflict was heart of entire prosecution
5. **Complete Inaction**: Not a single substantive motion filed

# IX. REQUESTED ACTION

Based on the evidence presented, the Office of Disciplinary Counsel should find that Mr. Tipp violated:

1. **MRPC Rule 1.1** - Failed to provide competent representation
2. **MRPC Rule 1.3** - Failed to act with reasonable diligence
3. **MRPC Rule 1.4** - Failed to adequately communicate with client
4. **MRPC Rule 8.4** - Engaged in conduct prejudicial to the administration of justice

The appropriate sanctions should reflect:

- Severity of constitutional violations ignored
- Complete abdication of professional duties
- Substantial prejudice to client
- Loss of civil remedies through malpractice

# X. CONCLUSION

Mr. Tipp's representation of Mr. Nuno represents a catastrophic failure of professional responsibility. His complete failure to identify Detective Brueckner's disqualifying conflict of interest - after the case was transferred due to another officer's conflict - demonstrates gross incompetence. His failure to file a single substantive motion despite overwhelming evidence of prosecutorial and police misconduct shows abandonment of his client's interests.

The Montana legal profession cannot tolerate such substandard representation, particularly when it results in conviction based on conflict-infected investigations and loss of substantial civil rights remedies. The Office of Disciplinary Counsel must act to protect future clients from similar harm.
