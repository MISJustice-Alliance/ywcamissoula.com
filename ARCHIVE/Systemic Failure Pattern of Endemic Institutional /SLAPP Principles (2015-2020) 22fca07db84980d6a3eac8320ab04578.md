## I. Understanding SLAPP and Its Application to Criminal Retaliation

### A. Definition and Core Characteristics

Strategic Lawsuits Against Public Participation (SLAPP) are legal actions filed not to win on the merits, but to burden defendants with litigation costs and intimidate them into silence[1](https://www.law.cornell.edu/wex/slapp_suit). While traditionally associated with civil defamation suits, the underlying principle—using legal process as a weapon to chill protected speech—applies equally to retaliatory criminal prosecutions[2](https://scholarship.law.upenn.edu/cgi/viewcontent.cgi?article=1774&context=jcl)[3](https://constitution.congress.gov/browse/essay/amdt1-7-2-5/ALDE_00013946/).

**Key SLAPP Characteristics Present in Nuno's Case:**

| SLAPP Element | Evidence in Nuno Case |
| --- | --- |
| **Timing** | Criminal charges filed immediately after protected speech (YWCA complaint)[4](https://theconversation.com/what-is-a-slapp-suit-legal-experts-explain-how-these-lawsuits-suppress-free-speech-251098) |
| **Forum Shopping** | Multiple jurisdictions weaponized (Seattle, Edmonds Municipal, Edmonds Superior Court, Lynwood,  Missoula) |
| **Spurious Claims** | "Stalking" charges for written complaint + donation[5](https://www.thejlawgroup.com/freedom-of-speech-and-understanding-slapp-strategic-lawsuits-against-public-participation) |
| **Escalating Tactics** | Dismissing/re-filing to increase bail |
| **Resource Drain** | Forced relocation, $150k career loss |
| **Chilling Effect** | Silenced legitimate grievances about harassment and civil rights violations |

### B. The Constitutional Intersection

SLAPP suits fundamentally violate the First Amendment's Petition Clause, which guarantees "the right of the people...to petition the government for a redress of grievances"[6](https://firstamendment.mtsu.edu/article/slapp-suits/). As Judge Colabella noted in *Gordon v. Marrone*: "Short of a gun to the head, a greater threat to First Amendment expression can scarcely be imagined"[6](https://firstamendment.mtsu.edu/article/slapp-suits/).

## II. Montana's 2025 UPEPA: A Game-Changer

### A. Statutory Framework

Montana's House Bill 292, enacted May 1, 2025, adopts the Uniform Public Expression Protection Act (UPEPA)[7](https://montanafreepress.org/2025/05/03/montana-joins-growing-list-of-states-with-codified-protection-against-frivolous-lawsuits/). This transforms Montana from having the nation's worst anti-SLAPP protections (0/100 score) to among the strongest[8](https://www.aclu.org/press-releases/in-narrow-ruling-supreme-court-revives-lawsuit-challenging-speech-related-retaliatory-arrest).

**Key UPEPA Provisions:**

1. **Broad Scope**: Protects "exercise of the right of freedom of speech or of the press, the right to assemble or petition...on a matter of public concern"[9](https://www.rcfp.org/anti-slapp-guide/washington/)
2. **Special Motion to Strike**: Filed within 60 days, automatically stays proceedings[7](https://montanafreepress.org/2025/05/03/montana-joins-growing-list-of-states-with-codified-protection-against-frivolous-lawsuits/)
3. **Burden Shifting**:
    - Movant shows cause of action based on protected activity
    - Respondent must establish prima facie case
    - Movant can still prevail showing defense as matter of law[10](https://antislapplaws.com/index.php?n=Site.DismissalStrikingPublicExpressionProtectionAntiSLAPP)
4. **Mandatory Fee Award**: Prevailing defendants recover attorney fees[7](https://montanafreepress.org/2025/05/03/montana-joins-growing-list-of-states-with-codified-protection-against-frivolous-lawsuits/)
5. **Immediate Appeal Right**: Denial of motion appealable[8](https://www.aclu.org/press-releases/in-narrow-ruling-supreme-court-revives-lawsuit-challenging-speech-related-retaliatory-arrest)

### B. Application to Criminal Retaliation

While UPEPA's text addresses "causes of action" (civil claims), its principles extend to criminal prosecutions used as SLAPP vehicles[11](https://www.uclalawreview.org/wp-content/uploads/2019/09/Zhang-Article-64-5.pdf):

1. **Retaliatory Prosecution Doctrine**: Supreme Court recognizes First Amendment bars prosecution in retaliation for protected speech (*Hartman v. Moore*)[12](https://www.law.cornell.edu/constitution-conan/amendment-1/retaliatory-prosecution-and-arrest)
2. **Abuse of Process**: Using criminal charges to silence critics constitutes malicious prosecution[2](https://scholarship.law.upenn.edu/cgi/viewcontent.cgi?article=1774&context=jcl)
3. **Objective Evidence Standard**: Recent Supreme Court guidance (*Gonzalez v. Trevino*, 2024) allows proving retaliation through comparative enforcement patterns[8](https://www.aclu.org/press-releases/in-narrow-ruling-supreme-court-revives-lawsuit-challenging-speech-related-retaliatory-arrest)

## IV. The Criminal SLAPP Phenomenon

## A. Recognition in Law

While anti-SLAPP statutes target civil suits, courts increasingly recognize criminal charges can serve identical censorship functions[11](https://www.uclalawreview.org/wp-content/uploads/2019/09/Zhang-Article-64-5.pdf):

1. **Objective Evidence Test**: If "no one has ever been arrested for engaging in a certain kind of conduct," retaliatory motive is presumed[12](https://www.law.cornell.edu/constitution-conan/amendment-1/retaliatory-prosecution-and-arrest)
2. **Comparative Non-Enforcement**: Showing similar conduct goes unprosecuted establishes discrimination[8](https://www.aclu.org/press-releases/in-narrow-ruling-supreme-court-revives-lawsuit-challenging-speech-related-retaliatory-arrest)
3. **Procedural Irregularities**: Bypassing normal channels indicates retaliatory intent[8](https://www.aclu.org/press-releases/in-narrow-ruling-supreme-court-revives-lawsuit-challenging-speech-related-retaliatory-arrest)
